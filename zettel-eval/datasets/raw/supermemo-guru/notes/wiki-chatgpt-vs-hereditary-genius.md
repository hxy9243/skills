## Hereditary Genius myth

The 19th century gave birth to Darwinian theory, and a misguided notion of the genetic inferiority of a subset of humans. It all started with [Galton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francis_Galton) 's *" [Hereditary genius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hereditary_Genius) "* (1869). Galton suggested that we should encourage reproduction of the gifted and sterilize those considered less able. He called for state intervention. Eugenics was born. To this day we hear remnants of that original reasoning when teachers speak of gifted and those "less fortunate".

Optimum education strategy is independent of the genes. All healthy kids should grow up in rich environments and be subject to free choice and complete absence of [coercion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercion_in_learning "Coercion in learning"). This truth can be derived from the principles of neuroscience. It can even be derived from the laws of physics that lead to the emergence of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") as the force of intelligence in humans.

Yet, many teachers firmly believe that "weaker" students require special attention, including the use of coercive tools for "assistance". The grain of truth is that people subject to prolonged coercion (at school or in prison) lose the skills needed for the adaptation to free life in select environments (see: [Brain is a perfectly adaptable device](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Brain_is_a_perfectly_adaptable_device "Brain is a perfectly adaptable device")).

Genetic basis of [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") is a notorious red herring that poisons the debate about the need to free the kids from the oppressive system of [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling").

Optimum education is based on **freedom** independent of the genes

## Harm of classification

The claim that kids differ wildly in their [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") is correct. All kids are different. There are many intelligences. Genes may affect preferences and trajectories, but they do not undermine the incredible human potential.

The harm of hereditary intelligence stratification comes from relegating a large subset of kids to a group of those with "less potential". This becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy. The child's potential gets suppressed, and the teaching community obtains more common-sense "evidence" that some kids are good for nothing.

In reality, the potential of all kids is maximized in the same way: [freedom to explore rich environments](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Optimization_of_behavioral_spaces_in_development "Optimization of behavioral spaces in development"). When kids follow their [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), they maximize their learning and maximize their ultimate [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence").

All healthy children are born with an amazing potential to develop high intelligence

## Inspiration from ChatGPT

ChatGPT boosted societal understanding of intelligence. Today, many people understand LLMs better than they understand the brain. This helps explain the insignificance of the genetic component in charting educational strategies. This helps explain the immense human potential at birth.

The power of ChatGPT rests in its vast knowledge. The quality of knowledge depends on the training data. The volume of knowledge depends on the number of parameters or neurons involved in training. Few people are interested in the speed of the processor(s) involved in the computation. Children can run DeepSeek from their own average PCs. Teens tinker with Llama on Raspberry Pi. Machine power does not translate directly to machine intelligence. Human brain is no different. Minor biological differences may matter only if they slow down or divert the learning process. The ultimate intelligence cares little about the life of individual neurons.

The architectures, models, processors, and other technical details matter for AI in the training phase. AI may need to consume the entire internet in weeks. However, from the point of view of the user, the speed of receiving the answer is secondary. It is the quality of answers that matters. The intelligence of a chatbot lies in the synaptic weights; not in the size of the memory, or the processing speed of the device that runs the query.

Synaptic weights add up to intelligence. We all have plenty to economize

## Genius passions

The same situation as in AI models, we have in humans. The human super-genius, [Terrence Tao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terence_Tao) has spent his entire life processing number, formulas, and proofs. No wonder his math brain is unmatched. But the main difference between Terrence and the rest of the world is that he became extremely passionate at the very early years. His obsession with math is equivalent to many years of heavy training on rich high-quality datasets. Can we suspect Terrence neurons are faster, more numerous, better connected, better supported? Is his brain architecture innately better? His neurons are probably similar to yours. Their number might be comparable. He developed the neurons, the connections, and the architecture optimum for processing math. Connectivity reflects knowledge which reflects training. Terrence developed a math-oriented connectivity. Terrence needed no specific genetic enhancement to soar. Most likely, the constellation of his polygenetic traits is lucky, but similar constellation can be found in many other humans. The obsession with math gave Terrence the unheard-of compute for training on math data. The obsession made Terrence.

The key to genius is passion that maximizes the use of human compute

## The Learn Drive

For humans, a powerful [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") is the key to [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"). Passionate kids that continue exploring and learning get the furthest. [Coercive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling") stands in the way, and this is the main differentiator. As some kids are more sensitive to traumatic experiences, they are first to be culled on their way to greatness. Even your [sex or skin color](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Myth:_Blacks_are_less_intelligent_than_whites "Myth: Blacks are less intelligent than whites") can lead to discrimination that will suppress or reorient your passions. Protruding ears can make you stand out and get put down. Genes do affect vulnerability.

Proportion and size of gray matter at birth, habituation rate, reaction time, and other parameters are used to predict future IQ of children (see: [IQ is a dismal measure of intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/IQ_is_a_dismal_measure_of_intelligence "IQ is a dismal measure of intelligence")). However, these are only differentiators that determine optimum adaptation niches (for survivability of the population). Their association with IQ is primarily exposed by the filtering aspect of coercive schooling. It is then taken as a fact that kids with fast reactions are smarter. Not at all. Einstein was a ruminant, not a cheetah. The ultimate outcome is the product of passion and time. The entire constellation of genetic variability interacts with the harmful interference from the environment, which may inhibit the incredible human ability to convert interests into intelligence.

## Personality

It is true that [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") depends on the personality. Optimum personality (curious, open, and agreeable) maximizes the pursuit of passions. Gene variants affect developmental trajectories and optimum adaptation. They do impact the ultimate personality. However, in rich and free environments, personalities adapt optimally, and the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") maximizes intelligence that serves the adaptation.

In contrast, in impoverished, discriminatory, oppressive or traumatic conditions, genetic differences may increase differentiation and the degree to which the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") is suppressed. As genes have much more impact on resilience, I discuss individual variants in [Great personalities do not come from genes](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Great_personalities_do_not_come_from_genes "Great personalities do not come from genes"). In there, I show that conducive conditions maximize human potential, which is amazing. The old rule applies: *Primum non nocere*.

Free and healthy environments minimize the impact of genes on personality and intelligence
