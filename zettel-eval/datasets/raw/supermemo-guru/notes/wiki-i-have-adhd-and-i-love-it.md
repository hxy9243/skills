## Criticism of SuperMemo Guru

I have received scathing criticism of my article: [Confusing creativity with ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Confusing_creativity_with_ADHD "Confusing creativity with ADHD"). The criticism came from a former user of [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo"), who suffers from ADHD, and for whom, the medical intervention was a blessing. Here is the text with the criticism: *" [Your article about ADHD is outrageously wrong and should be deleted](http://supermemopedia.com/wiki/Your_article_about_ADHD_is_outrageously_wrong_and_should_be_deleted) "*.

Below, I will try to explain that a label of ADHD encompasses the whole spectrum of underlying causes, and that symptoms used in classification of ADHD may include the derivatives of highly welcome properties of a healthy human brain.

The root cause of the entire misunderstanding is the problem with delineating a disease without the ability to dig deep into its neuropathology. The haziness of the definition of ADHD gave birth to an entire industry that channels kids into a narrow path of good performance at school. The forces that affect that industry are so numerous and complex that the phenomenon cannot easily be modelled and explained in a short text. True sufferers exist. [Creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity") leads to symptoms similar to those of ADHD. Big pharma promotes Ritalin. Many teachers want more Ritalin. Parents are tempted to use Ritalin. Healthy students use Ritalin to boost test performance. Diagnosis is on the rise. Mental health is on the decline. Many of those forces and trends have good and bad aspects. This is why my original article cannot be interpreted as *ADHD does not exist* (there is a link to the medical consensus is the text). It cannot be read as *Ritalin should be banned*. It cannot be understood as a claim of *global delusion with a phantom syndrome*. My relatively narrow message is that creative kids should not be bothered by complaints of teachers (or parents), should be cautious about drugs, and start from looking for simple remedies such as good sleep and exercise.

Before I compiled my remarks, some nice soul came to my defense. That defense text is worth reading because it carries some [interesting points I did not think of](http://supermemopedia.com/wiki/Talk:Your_article_about_ADHD_is_outrageously_wrong_and_should_be_deleted).

## The problem with psychiatry

Psychiatry is an ungrateful discipline. With an epidemic of mental disorders associated with the modern lifestyle, the role and the importance of psychiatric intervention keeps increasing. At the same time, psychiatry is under attack from many directions. Most of the attack is pretty uninformed, however, there is a major core problem in the center that keeps undermining the image and value of psychiatry.

Psychiatry is complex, hard, and ungrateful. It can be compared to an attempt to fix a car by watching its behavior, and then by changing the fuel mix

Without fully understanding the neurophysiological causes of each individual maladaptation, we will never arrive at a truly valid classification of psychiatric disorders. Without the ability to affect specific specialized brain structures and their specific receptor populations, we will never truly target the disease without shooting dozens of bystanders. Even seemingly harmless natural products like [melatonin](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Melatonin "Melatonin") work on different receptor populations. This way, applying a drug that works on one population of receptors or cells, produces unwelcome side effects in another population.

The best solution to the mental health disorder epidemic is prevention, which requires a major change in which society functions

It all begins in pregnancy. Stressed out moms under the influence of legal or prescribed psychoactive substances are the first step on the long road to derailing the marvel of the brain designed by millions of years of evolution. A third of modern births occurs via C-section depriving the kids of the precious vaginal microbial blanket. C-sections correlate with autism and a number of other disorders (incl. those affecting mental health). Newborns or toddlers are weaned early, or deprived of the breast entirely (see: [Daycare misery](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Daycare_misery "Daycare misery")). Much is said of nutritional or immune effects of breastfeeding, while the psychological effects might be most important.

Babies are ripped from their moms to spend their early days in institutional care. In daycare, a noticeable proportion of kids start showing behavioral problems, which we tend to mark as personality traits. Then a true "brain massacre" begins at the time of school. Within the first 2-3 years, joyful kids who crave learning, overwhelmingly turn into reluctant students. By the time they are teens, they preponderantly hate learning at school (see: [Why kids hate school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Why_kids_hate_school "Why kids hate school")). They may still love some aspects of schooling because peers and socializing are one of the best preventions against a further slide towards mental health issues. I meet kids at school who are suicidal due to a simple inability to see the world beyond grades and the reaction from teachers and parents. For years they are conditioned to respond to bells, rewards, and penalties dished out by the school system. Before they become adults, most have tried illegal drugs or abused alcohol.

When teens are to face adulthood, a mortal blow awaits: all the stuff they learned at school is of little use when it comes to getting a job. Their grades and qualifications often matter little. In formal terms, they lack experience. In brain terms, they are impotent when it comes to creativity and problem solving. This is another rejection from society that seem to whip a child in one direction only to penalize it for choosing wrong ways. When babies born today reach the age of 18, they will likely face mortal competition from an army of robots and tools of artificial intelligence. The acceleration is so tremendous that the school system will need to break and transform into a new beast. Otherwise humans will be taken care of by AI in a fashion in which we care of the endangered species today (hopefully not in a zoo).

A healthy fraction of modern adults finds their happy place in society. With a good job, good contribution to society, good social interaction, and a good chance to learn new things, happy mind gradually returns, and if there was any damage in childhood, it can gradually be compensated and largely forgotten.

We can make a great contribution to preventing a great deal of the mental slide by setting kids free. Let education be voluntary. Let it be a pleasure again. Homeschool, unschooling or democratic schooling are great ways to learn while staying free and happy. [Democratic schools](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Democratic_school "Democratic school") boast of negligible problems with psychiatric disorders because freedom makes kids thrive.

Sadly, psychiatry has a sad and monumentally harmful contribution to the enslavement of kids. [ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/ADHD "ADHD") is a disorder so poorly defined that even experts struggle or disagree on its individual subcomponents, their roles, the roots, the causes, etc. Dr Russell A. Barkley is a well-known expert on ADHD, sometimes criticized for his ties with big pharma. He suggested that *sluggish cognitive tempo* be separated from ADHD, and renamed to *"concentration deficit disorder"* (CDD). I meet all diagnostic criteria of ADHD except I do not see any of them as maladaptive. Just the opposite, all the symptoms I attribute to a good healthy brain in action. I instantly reject anything *"sluggish"* as a label for my own brain. See Barkley's reasoning: [Sluggish Cognitive Tempo/Concentration Deficit Disorder](http://www.oxfordhandbooks.com/view/10.1093/oxfordhb/9780199935291.001.0001/oxfordhb-9780199935291-e-9).

My cursory peek at Barkley's reasoning tells me he is probably right. My knowledge is insufficient to be sure. However, I can sure tell you that in a line of reasoning that does not name a single brain structure, receptor or neurohormone, it takes decades of learning and practice to make a good distinction. Few can get to Barkley's level of knowledge to make a good judgement. Experts are cryptic and they have a right to be wrong. Where diagnosis is based on questionnaires and influenced by opinions from parents and teachers, it is a fertile ground for the entire industry to be born. The ADHD industry thrives on a key premise: **make kids ready for school**. When I was at the age of around ten, doctors considered medicating the little me. I had been too disruptive at school. I am not sure how I got off the hook, but I might not be writing these words today. Perhaps I would comply with the school regimen, do my [homework](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homework "Homework"), and graduate with good grades. I wanted to study medicine but switched to biology. One of the main reasons was that biology offered more freedom and lower standards! Yes. I wanted lower standards in my college. Perhaps a medicated me would dutifully cram my way through a medical school and now, decades later, consider medicating other children. If it worked for me, I would sure be inclined to believe it would work for others.

## Dr Bruce Perry on ADHD

I don't mind standing in opposition to a "hundred psychiatrists" as long as I stand in the same line as people with an incredible insight into how the brain works.

Bruce Perry is an expert on childhood trauma. He is one of those I am proud to stand with. He participated in research on Columbine. He knows innumerable cases of kids in trouble, and can see those emotional feedback loops that can consume entire households. In his writings he deplores the arrival of massive [daycare](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Daycare_misery "Daycare misery") and early schooling. He noticed that just a few generations ago, one kid was taken care of by 4-5 adults. Today, on an assembly line, the proportions reverse. By the time of kindergarten nobody is dismayed by the occasional 1:20 (i.e. twenty kids per one tired guardian). Perry is unique in his understanding of the multithreaded development of brain structures and skills in a child. His unique perspective comes from working with children damaged by abuse at different stages and by a different "method". This lead to the Neurosequential Model of Therapeutics, which is based on deconvoluting the complexity of psychiatric assessment in cases of abuse.

Perry is also an avid critic of ADHD overdiagnosis and psychiatric overmedication. If I was to translate Perry's philosophy to a simple language, it would be:

" *Love is more important than Ritalin* " (Dr Bruce Perry paraphrased)

For an adult with ADHD, the truth is obviously not universal, but for parents with troubled kids, it is (on average).

To the need for love, I would yet add freedom to roam and exercise. On second thoughts though, Perry provides a sufficient condition. No loving parent can possibly overlook a child's need to move around freely. Motor restriction might be one of the key factors that underlie the explosion of ADHD symptoms at school.

Dr Perry said:

> ADHD is best thought of as a description. Any one of us at any given time would fit at least a couple of those criteria

I go further. I claim that I fit all the criteria at times, and some of the criteria most of the time. By the relaxed standards of DSM-5, I am allegedly a case of ADHD.

A site devoted to adult ADHD posted a [condemnation of Dr Perry](https://aadduk.org/2014/04/02/dr-bruce-perry-adhd-is-not-a-real-disease) for his remarks. The post and the comments have many parallels with that [critical mail I received](http://supermemopedia.com/wiki/Your_article_about_ADHD_is_outrageously_wrong_and_should_be_deleted). The predominant emotion is anger with someone who claims to be an expert and allegedly is unable to sense the pain and stigma of ADHD. Comparison to David Beckham is supposed to be uncomplimentary.

I cannot speak for Dr Perry, but I need to stress that his brain expertise is stellar, while his professional life and conduct are the best testimony to his being a staunch and passionate defender of children.

When commenting on Perry's claims published in the [Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/society/2014/mar/30/children-hyperactivity-not-real-disease-neuroscientist-adhd), Dr Marilyn Wedge, a proponent of drug-free childhood, wrote in [Psychology Today](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/suffer-the-children/201404/adhd-does-not-exist-part-2):

> To say \[that symptoms of ADHD\] are a disease entity is like saying that a man who comes to the doctor's office with chest pain and sweating has a "fever." The fever can be treated with medication, but what about the underlying cause of the fever? Fever is a symptom, not a disease

The she added:

> If a small fraction of the resources spent on finding biological causes for ADHD were used for researching the psychological and social causes of the symptoms, there would be real progress on finding ways to help over-active and distracted children without drugging them. Dr. Perry is a leader in this new direction for scientific research

I would add that we still need to decode the mechanisms that generate rampant creativity, hyperactive behavior, laser-sharp attention, motor modulation of the thought process, and the optimization of the mental computation in problem solving (see: [How to solve any problem?](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/How_to_solve_any_problem%3F "How to solve any problem)). These are all vital ingredients of creative lifestyle. The same ingredients may wax and wane in ADHD, or just go haywire. With all that multidirectional ramifications, I am not surprised the debate is hot and is not likely to end soon. If I was ever to face the dilemma of medication, I would rather fall back on *Primum non nocere*.

## ADHD at school

One of my teen friends from the football field stands out in intelligence and odd behavior. His name is Matti. He is the loudest and clearly hyperactive. He can be an annoyance. He promises fair play and then shoots at the goal like mad without consideration for preschoolers, girls, and people in spectacles. Many kids declare they do not like Matti (not too convincingly). Others love him to death: *"He is the funniest guy around. He would do anything for a joke"*. He is one of very few kids in the area who speak some English.

Just as I was writing about the problem of ADHD overdiagnosis. Matti whispered that he has been diagnosed with ADHD. I was pretty stunned. I could see a classic case of a kid with an incredible potential who was somehow labelled with a psychiatric disorder. Even worse, Matti was heavily medicated for a longer while. His behavior at school improved dramatically on medication. This is a typical good reason for the adult world to love ADHD drugs. Luckily, the medication did not feel good to Matti himself: "I was like a zombie. I would go to school, do my homework, and go to sleep. I was not living. I was sleepwalking. Such life is no fun". He refused to continue with the medication and his mom consented. He is back to his creatively crazy self.

When I asked Matti about his passions, interests and plans for the future, I was amazed how broad his thinking was. He stood 2-4 years ahead of his age. Form his school bag, he pulled out a painting: "I did it at school today. Classes were pretty boring" (see the picture). I was amazed. Here is a kid with multiple talents, spending all his day at school doodling, while taxpayers pay for this teacher to drone his broadcast into the unreceptive air. Instead of pursuing his interest, he is locked in a system, and labelled as trouble-making ADHD. Instead of freedom to learn, he disrupts life at school making a great deal of people unhappy. Doodling and waste are still better than coercion. If his teachers pushed Matti to do what the kid hates, his ADHD symptoms would explode. I asked Matti, if he thought of [homeschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeschooling "Homeschooling"). He was not sure what homeschooling was but was instantly interested. He said he would google the concept and talk to his parents.

Matti calls himself lazy. I tried to get to the root cause of his "laziness" and concluded this is just a label he received at school. Matti is lucky to have parents who give him a great deal of freedom and the label does not get reinforced at home. In a longer interview, I pointed to his strengths, and how scrupulous and un-lazy he can be. How fast he can learn with enthusiasm, etc. In addition, the conversation gave me an impression I am talking to a very wise and well-focused young man. I stress that "well-focused" only because of the labels he received. I rarely pay attention. When a conversation is fast-flowing, and informative, I just call it "productive".

## ADHD drugs, and Alzheimer's

People like [Donald Trump](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Donald_Trump "Donald Trump") tend to double down under pressure. When my words get criticized, instead of getting humble, I rush to seek more support for my models. I tend to go into more controversy. This is [natural and healthy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Brains_algorithms_protect_models_of_reality "Brains algorithms protect models of reality")! When I say [Education counteracts evolution](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Education_counteracts_evolution "Education counteracts evolution"), I insist that natural resistance is part of exploratory algorithm that improves the creative aspects of learning. The criticism would soften my stance if it could point to an error, esp. a glaring error that I should be able to see with shame. Unfortunately, more often, I go in search for support for my words, and when I find it, I double down like Trump. Elsewhere I explain the [value of wrong models](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Value_of_wrong_models "Value of wrong models") and how confirmation bias sifts away evidence that contradicts the model. This is why adding strength to a model is always good, even if it is to turn out wrong decades later.

Hereby, for web archeologists, I will make an outrageously bold prediction. Ritalin improves attention. It does so by tempering defenses against incoherent knowledge. As such it leads to a degree of [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") to resolve [neural conflict](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/War_of_the_networks "War of the networks"). By increasing incongruent input of knowledge, medicated individual will suffer an increased risk of Alzheimer's disease. I was born a bit arrogant, and this is why I am sure I am right. Future will tell. I should add, however, that if positive outcomes of the drug vastly outweigh the negatives, the mere "happy mind" effect might produce the opposite result. In other words, the research needs to weight up the two: the overload damage to the networks, vs. the healing effect of reduced stress. In addition, [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity") might be neuroprotective via its impact on the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"). In other words, in cases where symptoms of ADHD are a result of prolifically creative mind, the risk of Alzheimer's might be lower in the first place.

Combating ADHD with medication may suppress the benefits that come with a creative mind
