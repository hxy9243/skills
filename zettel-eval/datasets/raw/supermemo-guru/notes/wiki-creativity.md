## Definition

**Creativity** is the ability to produce new ideas. The term **creativity** can be used as a skill or as a process. **Creativity** as a skill enables an effective search for creative associations. **Creative thinking** is a process in which remote ideas are associated to produce new quality. When we say: *this kid is creative*, we mean that her brain is able to produce new value in a speedy, rich, or original manner.

## Mechanism

**Creativity** as a process is a systematic search of memory space with a purpose of finding a useful association. Creativity can also be spontaneous and disruptive when the explorations happen despite an effort to focus on something else.

The brain enters a creative mode upon any form of inspiration. The inspiration can come from a piece of knowledge (news, reading, etc.), sensory stimulus, memory [recall](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Recall "Recall"), etc. In the creative mode, disruptive random signaling in the brain increases. This comes from the activation of the search mode in which the brain looks for new associations in problem solving. The problem to solve may be an insignificant incongruity in knowledge, or a new idea inspired by any form of disruption, or an active search for a missing piece in a complex problem's jigsaw puzzle of knowledge. The most obvious component of creativity is the random search. This search requires a different mode of activations in the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network"). Individual [concepts](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept") much increase the probability of activating nearby concepts even when links between concepts are weak or absent. A weak link is a link with low valence, while an absent link may have a form of a silent synapse.

In creative mode, random search of the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") seeks atomic dyadic [concept](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept") links that may constitute a solution to a problem. If a new concept link forms a solution, the brain gets a loud notification that we call an " **aha moment** ". This is the time the brain creates new value: new [generalization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Generalization "Generalization"), new conclusion, new solution, etc. This moment is highly rewarding and powers further creative explorations in a rewarded individual.

In creative mode, random search of the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") seeks [concept](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept") associations that may reveal a solution to a problem

## Creativity vs. focus

**Creativity** and **attention** are in a perpetual competition. While attention guides a deductive process of thinking. Creativity is divergent and produces unpredictable activations in many areas of the brain (or different areas of knowledge). For details see: [Creativity cycle](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity_cycle "Creativity cycle")

When kids are coerced to do school chores, they show substantial [reactance](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Reactance "Reactance"). The healthier the kid, the higher the reactance. A child may seem distracted, [fidgeting](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Fidgeting "Fidgeting"), looking for excuses, stubborn, bored, mind-wandering, etc. This is why reactant kids are easily labelled as "having a problem". This is how high **creativity** contributes to ADHD overdiagnosis (see: [Confusing creativity with ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Confusing_creativity_with_ADHD "Confusing creativity with ADHD")). While teachers or parents look for punitive or psychiatric remedies, there is often an instant remedy available at hand: let the child learn what she wants.

In healthy individuals, [free learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning") provides an instant remedy for disruptive creativity

## Loss of creativity at school

George Land noticed that creativity drops by one measure from [98% to 2%](https://www.youtube.com/watch) as we grow up. This is often attributed to aging. This can also be attributed to brain [conceptualization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptualization "Conceptualization") in normal development. In reality, the main contributor to that loss is [schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schooling "Schooling"). As creativity is powered by the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), the damage inflicted by [coercive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_learning "Coercive learning") can be reversed over time with unconstrained [free learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning"). See: [Schools suppress the learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schools_suppress_the_learn_drive "Schools suppress the learn drive")

[Free learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning") is the best prevention of creative injury at school

## Creative mode

Stochastic search initiated in the creative mode is hard to investigate. At this point we can probably only hypothesize how it is implemented in the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network"). It will definitely affect the way [spreading activation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spreading_activation "Spreading activation") works. It will also definitely be able to employ weak or silent links. The switch from the focused mode to the creative mode would be equivalent to a transition from a deductive use of high [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") connections, to a stochastic use of the entire spectrum of available connections (incl. silent connections).

There are a few hypothetical mechanisms that might be involved:

- **neural**: specific signals are sent throughout the brain to change the way activations spread (e.g. [default mode network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Default_mode_network) might be involved in that process)
- **modal**: changes in the mode of signaling seem attractive (e.g. activation frequencies could play that role)
- **neurohormonal**: jolts of inspiration have a definite neurohormonal impact on creativity, and so does coffee

It is likely that all above mechanisms are involved. It is important that the creative mode reaches the most remote areas of the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network"). This is why the **modal** hypothesis is very attractive. **Neurohormonal** regulation may be too blunt unless it can also operate diffusely at the **modal** level. **Neural** modulation would require complex connectivity. Its role, however, might show up when bridging remote corners of the cortex in the same way as we can observe it in the operations of the hippocampus.

In " [Fidgeting is good](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Fidgeting_is_good "Fidgeting is good") ", I hypothesize on the origins of fidgeting. If the suggested mechanism was correct, we would know that the **neural** mechanism of creativity would be non-specific and imprecise. It would be capable of spilling into extrinsic networks such as the motor control systems.

## Creativity vs. expertise

Creativity tends to drops with expertise. This happens when [fast thinking](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Fast_thinking "Fast thinking") disposes of the need for creative slow thinking. However, a simple remedy to that drop is diversified learning (e.g. [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading") or [neural creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neural_creativity "Neural creativity")). There is a close link between new learning, [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), and creativity. To retain high degree of creativity, experts must employ lifelong learning. For details see: [Knowledge in creative problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_in_creative_problem_solving "Knowledge in creative problem solving").

## Value of creative output

Some authors attach a condition of "high value of output" to the definition of creativity. However, this quality requirement implies an external assessor of value. The definition used throughout [this site](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo_Guru "SuperMemo Guru") is based solely on the neural and neurohormonal modes in which the brain operates. The creative process does not need to come up with a high-value output. Equally well, it may come up with irrelevant associations.
