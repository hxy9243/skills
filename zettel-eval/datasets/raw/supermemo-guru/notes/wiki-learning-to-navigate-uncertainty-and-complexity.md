## Fearless child

A healthy child has no fears of the surrounding world of knowledge. It knows no concept of [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory"). Child's knowledge is spotty and incomplete, the world is full of uncertainly, and its complexity seems insurmountable. A young pristine ignorant brain takes on this challenge unfazed. That composure and confidence will be systematically undermined at school.

To navigate in this world full of contradictions and surprises, the child will soak in information, and massively employ [forgetting](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Forgetting "Forgetting") in the process of bold [generalization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Generalization "Generalization"). The model of reality will slowly emerge and crystallize. At first, the model is full of gaping holes, and it is [consistent](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Consistent "Consistent") only in small subsets of the semantic net of knowledge.

## Harm of direct instruction

The problem of learning at school, begins in the very first days of academic training. Whenever kids play, explore, visit a museum, or watch a theatre play, they are in control of the flow of information that registers in their mind. Academic instruction: letters, numbers, counting and reading are too abstract to be taken in in a [coherent](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherent "Coherent") and [mnemonic](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Mnemonic "Mnemonic") manner. The [curriculum](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curriculum "Curriculum") has its demands, and many a teacher won't have patience to see a child employ [generalization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Generalization "Generalization") to grow a number sense by watching chicken in a farm. With love, good intent, and smiles, the teacher comes in with an inevitable degree of coercion and demands a straight association: *"This is number 8. See those two little circles. This is how we write the number 8. Number 8 comes after 7"*.

[Direct instruction](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Direct_instruction "Direct instruction") replaces [generalization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Generalization "Generalization") that should be employed in the emergence of the number sense. All children get is a straight association. This deprives skeletal memories of finding an optimum location in the structure of knowledge that would maximize [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence") and maximize the [mnemonic power](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Mnemonic_power "Mnemonic power") of the context. Instead, we may develop flimsy association that may be easily forgotten. A weak association will also [interfere](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interfere "Interfere") with other memories. With any form of pressure to know, this kind of shaky memory structure can lead to [toxic memories](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory"). We all know those flimsy associations from [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo"). Every student makes this mistake early in the process: overdoing memorization. This is how we all discover [leeches](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Leech "Leech"), and then use our metacognition to avoid the pitfalls of bad memories.

With a bit of practice, we can ease in into a perfectly coherent, and pleasurable learning process based on [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading"). Unlike classic [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo"), [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading") is a perfect metaphor for good learning. That classic memorization variant at school is reminiscent of conditioning that Pavlov applied to his dogs. It is great to develop a large number of memories in a short period of time, but it is awful if the goal is to achieve knowledge that contributes to [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence").

## Errors of generalization

In a happy class of first graders, a teacher may experience a shock. After one year of drills, one of the children writes the number 5, but that number is wrong! It is its own mirror image. The shock causes consternation and stress for everyone involved in the system. What went wrong? The teacher feels like a failure. Parents are ashamed of not supervising [homework](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homework "Homework"). The headmaster is jittery about the image of the school. How could this have happed, after hours of handwriting and handwriting homework.

Instead, everyone should celebrate. The mirror image of 5 is an indication the kid was not [cramming](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Cramming "Cramming"). He was [generalizing](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Generalization "Generalization"). The explanation appears simple. In the classroom, the delivery is passive. There is hardly any time for the teacher to go bench to bench and see if the kids actively memorized the correct shape of numbers. Even if they do, young memories are in the flow. Even ten successful reproductions does not guarantee the eleventh. The [homework](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homework "Homework") is even worse, the kids can see the template number, and reproduce it below. Sometimes several times mindlessly as if being passive ten times is much better than being passive once. Mirror image [generalization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Generalization "Generalization") is unlikely because the kid receives the template in the right shape. It is easy to be a copy cat, even while daydreaming.

In [computer games](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Computer_games "Computer games"), number 5 is gold. You cannot have much fun in games without a good number sense. If you hit the keyboard, 5 will always come up right. It is never a mirror image. Without the intervention of school, the same child, 50 year later, might ask with surprise: does it really matter if 5 is written one way, not the other?

Our whole [model](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Model "Model") of reality is full of similar simplifications. Metacognitive adults will look up dictionaries and Wikipedia to soak in perfect correct definitions. However, minor misconceptions may survive for decades or for life. The confrontation with reality is the usual way of discovery. False knowledge that brings no penalty becomes low priority knowledge error.

Naturally, we need to raise the bar of precision when sending out multibillion dollar space probes. Mixing up imperial and metric units may be tantamount to a crime of negligence. Our first grader, living in a world without handwriting, might actually work for NASA and survive with the implied belief that 5 does not need to be written with a belly to the right. With a mirror 5 [generalization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Generalization "Generalization"), the space probe might actually do well. Nearly all its function is digital, and human perception of 5 is less relevant.

## Creativity at school

We can see then that instead of solving problems, kids memorize. Instead of navigating through a complex world full of uncertainty, they are served perfectly [crystallized knowledge](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_crystallization "Knowledge crystallization") ready for mindless memorization. There is nothing wrong with memorizing facts. However, the pathway to memory will determine the quality of memory. Only the natural [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") can provide a good chance for an optimum fit. The law of optimum [learntropy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learntropy "Learntropy") is particularly important before metacognitive skills arm a child with full mastery of her brain powers. Without the [fundamental law of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Fundamental_law_of_learning "Fundamental law of learning") there is no true build up of [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence").

## The impact of schooling on intelligence

In the process of [schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schooling "Schooling"), we crank up the mass production of adults who believe they must possess the skills of massive mindless memorization of sequences. When this belief is combined with sufficient social pressure and fragile personality, we grow unhappy societies with rampant increase in mental disease. Instead, we should let kids focus on solving problems, making their own decisions, and choosing their own path through knowledge. This is the opposite of the demands of the [curriculum](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curriculum "Curriculum"). Kids that navigate complexity and uncertainty will not easily be turned into the [Harari's *"useless class"*](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Harari "Harari") in the wake of the arrival of [artificial intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence").
