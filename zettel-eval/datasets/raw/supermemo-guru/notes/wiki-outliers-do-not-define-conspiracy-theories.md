## Mocking innovation

Outliers do not define conspiracy theories. Copernicus once looked like an outlier. Continental drift theory once looked like a conspiracy theory.

Verdicts depend on data. Each human has access to, and remembers, different subsets of knowledge.

[Diversity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Diversity "Diversity") of knowledge is humanity's strength. A [homogenizing school system](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_of_schooling "Problem of schooling") erodes that strength and undermines our collective intelligence.

I know that [school coercion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_learning "Coercive learning") causes [dyslexia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dyslexia "Dyslexia"), yet that claim is today easily branded as conspiracy thinking (see: [Dyslexia Conspiracy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dyslexia_Conspiracy "Dyslexia Conspiracy")). I don't mind; solid evidence wins in the end, even against powerful vested interests (e.g. the school system).
