## Definition

**Executive function** is a set of cognitive skills that can be employed in a successful execution of tasks.

## Executive deficit

When executive deficit shows up, we look for remedies, e.g. via behavioral therapy. When those come up short, we look for pathology. Executive deficit may be the first step to the diagnosis of [ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/ADHD "ADHD").

The term executive function is abused in the name of controlling children. When children fail to work on tasks imposed by adults, we may blame **executive function deficit**. In reality, execution is a reflection of motivation, which is a reflection of autonomy and [interest](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interest "Interest").

## Commercial interest

Many books have been written with a purpose of controlling children. A reputable scientist with extensive knowledge may dispense advice on how to improve the **executive function**, and this kind of knowledge is gobbled up by parents who have already set plans for their child's life. In this kind of literature, **executive function** is a codeword for obedience. When a child executes a command without hesitation, his executive function is said to be solid.

## Formula for good executive

The formula for a fantastically strong executive is autonomy. If knowledge is built via the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), the strong goals emerge naturally. Those natural goals are rooted in an extensive [knowledge valuation network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation_network "Knowledge valuation network"). [Concept](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept") nodes in such a network are richly connected and carry high valuations. When goals are clear and sturdy, passionate executive is just a matter of freedom. In an autonomous individual, there are no issues with motivation, [self-discipline](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Self-discipline "Self-discipline") and the **executive function**. That executive strength is just a matter of time and grows in proportion to knowledge, which lives in a positive feedback loop with exuberant [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"). **Executive function** suffers greatly in [sleep deprivation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep_deprivation "Sleep deprivation"), however, free individuals are likely to deploy [free running sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_running_sleep "Free running sleep"). If the brain is a device that tends to accumulate errors in the course of the day, optimum **executive function** should be detected in the early hours of the morning (assuming [good sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Good_sleep "Good sleep")).

In healthy people, passion is an instant remedy for any executive function deficit

## Executive skills

The term **executive skills** was popularized in books by Dr Peg Dawson. Dawson knows a great deal of psychology, however, her writing is clearly directed at developing **executive skills** needed to perform well at school. This means performing well on tasks that may not be the derivative of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"). These are tasks coercively imposed by the school system. These are not **executive skills** needed to develop a breakthrough drug for cancer. These are skills needed to execute household chores and boring [homework](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homework "Homework").

I can now show that **executive skills** may easily be rephrased as natural skills that require no training and no coaching. Those natural **executive skills** require only one condition: [intrinsic motivation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intrinsic_motivation "Intrinsic motivation") (e.g. passion for building model airplanes, or similar).

Dawson is aware of the natural approach presented in this text. This is why she expands the definition of **executive skills** to require that they must involve: distractibility, fatigue, or boredom.

However, those constraints make any coaching in **executive skills** potentially harmful. Any attempt to override the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), [boredom](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Boredom "Boredom") or fatigue must be seen as an attempt to combat natural defenses deployed by the brain against [inefficient computing](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptual_computation "Conceptual computation"), and neural injury.

**Distractibility** is the presence of a signal of higher [learntropy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learntropy "Learntropy"). Ideally, that new signal should take over the focus of [attention](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Attention "Attention"). Any training that combats that natural behavior will result in the loss of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"), inability to discriminate against [asemantic learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Asemantic_learning "Asemantic learning"), and more.

**[Boredom](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Boredom "Boredom")** is simply low [learntropy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learntropy "Learntropy"). A brain trained to tolerate boredom will be a brain that will tolerate time-wasting. A great deal of criticism of [videogames](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Videogames "Videogames") comes from their ability to provide high [learntropy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learntropy "Learntropy") signal. Instead of celebrating the new educational opportunity, we mourn the loss of the status of [schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schooling "Schooling") in the eyes of the young generation (see: [Videogames are better than teachers](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Videogames_are_better_than_teachers "Videogames are better than teachers")).

**Fatigue** is a signal for the brain that it should rest. Surviving fatigue is not a skill, it is a [bad habit learned at school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/50_bad_habits "50 bad habits")! Surviving fatigue may save lives if you are a surgeon or a pilot, but the right strategy is not coaching, but avoiding work in a fatigued condition. We do not coach drivers to perform well while intoxicated. We just ask them to take a walk.

**Executive functions** frequently mentioned in the context of coaching are: task initiation, sustained attention, task switching, impulse control and [working memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Working_memory "Working memory"). It is easy to show that all come naturally or are not usefully trainable.

### Task initiation

It may be hard to initiate learning for a math test. However, if a child is passionate about building a robot, he will wake up earlier than usual to initiate the task out of sheer passion. **For tasks we love, initiation is instant**.

### Sustained attention

It may be hard to keep reading a boring classic novel needed for a test. However, if a child is passionate about Harry Potter, she may read continually late in the night until her lids drop in natural slumber. **For tasks we love, attention is laser sharp and undivided**.

### Task switching

It may be hard to switch from a Netflix movie to [cramming](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Cramming "Cramming") grammar for a test. However, the reverse may be instant. That boring school book can be tossed into a dusty corner, so that the kid could devour new tricks of Roblox on YouTube. **We switch to tasks we love with ease**.

### Impulse control

If a nagging parent interrupts a gamer with a call to [homework](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homework "Homework") (or even dinner), she may experience an emotional eruption that will seem to hint at poor impulse control (see: [Reward deprivation in addictions](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Reward_deprivation_in_addictions "Reward deprivation in addictions")). However, when the same kid is jumping around in joy while installing Linux for the first time in her life, she may exhibit emotions that better be left uncurbed. Joy and passion will make the task cognitively easy. The benefits will increase. Happy productivity leaves a healthy trace of [well-stabilized](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") and [coherent knowledge](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence") in memory. **The problem of impulse control is negligible in conditions of freedom**.

### Working memory

Extensive [working memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Working_memory "Working memory") may be wrongly used as a marker of [self-discipline](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Self-discipline "Self-discipline"). In reality, extensive working memory is often just the sustained ability to load [incoherent](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence") or [asmenatic](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Asemantic_learning "Asemantic learning") knowledge to memory on demand.

[Working memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Working_memory "Working memory") is a notorious culprit in student's low self-esteem. When an obedient student is made to study hard for weeks on end, she will develop all the necessary knowledge structures that lighten the burden of working memory. In addition, sustained attention will make it easy to juggle pieces of knowledge when solving problems. However, all that knowledge scaffolding is likely to be poorly coherent, poorly semantic, and prone to [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference"). This knowledge is likely to be promptly [forgotten](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Forgetting "Forgetting"). [Problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving") becomes algorithmic and robotic. An obedient student will complete the job and the teacher will praise good **executive function**. She will also praise good [working memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Working_memory "Working memory"). In contrast, a creative kid who cannot stand coercion at school will build no scaffolding, little understanding, his working memory will seem dysfunctional, and coercion will result in no progress. That lack of progress is a defensive blessing. [Working memory training may reduce neurogenesis](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Working_memory_training_may_reduce_neurogenesis "Working memory training may reduce neurogenesis")). This seeming stagnation spares knowledge that is needed to retain healthy [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") (see: [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness")). Working memory tasks tested at school may bear little resemblance to tasks we encounter in real life when facing real problems. Instead of training working memory, we should rather enhance it with [stable long-term knowledge](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") that makes [computation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptual_computation "Conceptual computation") easy. The hardest problems faced by mankind may take decades to solve. The solution is not likely to depend on the extent of anybody's working memory. See: [Working memory is not trainable](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Working_memory_is_not_trainable "Working memory is not trainable").

### Bondage

Drs Peg Dawson and Richard Guare wrote a book *"Smart but scattered"*. The book shows how to use reward and penalty to help children self-organize. However, I see it differently. The term *"scattered children"* can be interpreted as children who escape the controlling grip of the parent. They scatter through the fingers of the coercive hand.

Coaching in **executive skills** for the sake of improving school performance may be paraphrased as *" **Do what I tell you** \[task initiation\], **continue without a break** \[sustained attention\], **do not do anything else** \[task switching\], **do not complain** \[impulse control\], and **combat fatigue** by clenching your teeth \[working memory overload\](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/due to [homeostatic sleepiness](https:/supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeostatic_sleepiness "Homeostatic sleepiness"))"*.

**Executive skill training** is aimed at controlling children

## Prefrontal lobes

The [prefrontal lobes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prefrontal_cortex) are the source of many control signals associated with **executive function**. However, in books on [self-discipline](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Self-discipline "Self-discipline") or [schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schooling "Schooling"), the prefrontal cortex is rarely evoked to explain important practical conclusions. Prefrontal lobes are often mentioned to explain why children are too immature to make their own decisions. If **executive skills** and the frontal lobes take 30 years to develop, the implication is that the adults must assist up to that point of maturity. *"Immature prefrontal lobes"* become a codeword for [coercion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercion "Coercion") that masquerades as necessary assistance. For an example of a pseudoscientific invocation of the frontal lobes see: [Victoria Dunckley is my nightmare](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Victoria_Dunckley_is_my_nightmare "Victoria Dunckley is my nightmare").

Part of the cortical maturation process is based on synaptic pruning. Peg Dawson's interpretation is that a child should do math rather than [videogames](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Videogames "Videogames"), so that the right connections are pruned. Instead, the correct reasoning is that the process of pruning is optimum when the learning choices are based on the signal [learntropy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learntropy "Learntropy"). Only the child's brain can make such learntropic decisions (see: [Optimality of the learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Optimality_of_the_learn_drive "Optimality of the learn drive")). With the learntropy criterion, a videogame will serve a child better than a math test. The latter may, for example, lead to [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory"). See: [Videogames are better than teachers](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Videogames_are_better_than_teachers "Videogames are better than teachers").

## Coaching

The premise of coaching intended to "improve" the **executive function** is:

> Neurological maturation isn't a set process that just unfolds according to a pre-determined genetic plan. The upshot for parents is that coaching and structured interventions can have a hugely positive impact, particularly when a child is stuck

This premise requires rephrasing to express the true nature of the [conceptualization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptualization "Conceptualization") process in development:

Neurological maturation is a process that unfolds via natural adaptation to the enviornment

## My own story

I immodestly insist that my **executive function** is excellent. I live my life along the [Plan](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Plan "Plan") in [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo"). This requires meticulous planning of the day down to a minute. I run barefoot ultramarathons and swim in winter. Both require a great deal of [self-discipline](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Self-discipline "Self-discipline"). However, my formula for good **executive function** is not "training". The formula for good executive is gradual and incremental improvement towards better understanding of one's own biological and psychological needs. I struggled with self-discipline as a student, and solved the problem with [micro-rules of productivity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Micro-rules_of_productivity "Micro-rules of productivity"). These rules allow of small steps in the direction of healthy self-discipline. The magic behind this formula for self-discipline based on self-knowledge is that all difficult things can be made easy when we find out how to make them fun. It may take years or even decades! Once self-discipline becomes pleasurable, it is no longer self-discipline. It is happy living. See: [Simple formula for a happy life](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Simple_formula_for_a_happy_life "Simple formula for a happy life").

In high school, due to coercion, I got to hate Polish literature. Today, if you ask me to read a book in classic Polish literature, I would not survive for more than a few minutes (see: [Why I do not read fiction](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Why_I_do_not_read_fiction "Why I do not read fiction")). If I forced myself to sustain reading (e.g. for monetary rewards), I would probably read without comprehension. I would vocalize the sentences with my mind busy over something else (e.g. looping over *"How awful that book is. I understand nothing"*). What does it say about my **executive function**? Precisely nothing. My **executive function** is great for things I love, and disastrously bad for things I dislike. This stark discrimination is what we lose through the years of [schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schooling "Schooling").

When a coach says *"A task like writing a term paper places heavy demands on the executive function"*, I instantly think of writing my own texts for [SuperMemo Guru](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo_Guru "SuperMemo Guru"). Invariably, I can't wait to start. Pity the days are so short.

Instead of writing long books on how to improve **executive function** in children, we should just set them free and let them develop their own interests and passions.

Overtime, in healthy individuals, freedom makes **executive function** a non-issue
