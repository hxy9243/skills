Rather than focusing on the Primer, I’m finding a more powerful framing to be in centering the essay around scaling high-growth environments.

- You can read a book about starting a startup, or you can join [Y Combinator](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z9MxCsvv8DcHndqRECbxrRi). Which do you choose? This is the power of an [Enabling environment](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z492hGrHvRvJiEY9UfB4Mby).
	- The latter’s a much more effective growth environment, and it’s mostly not the funding.
		- When you read the book, you’re a founder wanna-be reading about the activities of founders, and you’re not “practicing founding”; when you do YC, you’re *doing founding,* in a community of founders. The book’s abstract, but YC’s not: every lesson is in the context of the idea you care deeply about:
		- [Enabling environments’ activities directly serve an intrinsically meaningful purpose](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z3PJFWDZ7gar2ttawQTmBek)
		- [Enabling environments focus on doing what’s enabled](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z2etsLyP1LJUwNDPCwvRdUG)
		- [Enabling environments focus on creating opportunities for growth and action, not on skill-building](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z7d63BYfJrd81VFE25jkcDd)
		- The difference isn’t just emotional: you’re going to learn this material much more effectively in the context of YC because it’s a [Participatory environment](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zRWEWHx4cQyqQWRh26gp7ad): [Participatory environments support learning](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zU3Cw3dParuur9TFVNJMiqS)
		- The format also allows its designers to author founders’ experience over a multi-month period, while [Mass mediums mostly lack an authored time dimension beyond a day](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zBcm1RNbzedjDMTt8rRxoWt)
		- The authentic purpose doesn’t just improve the experience for participating founders: it also helps YC’s creators design much better systems.
		- [Powerful enabling environments usually arise as a byproduct of projects pursuing their own intrinsically meaningful purposes](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z2huUCj3ko99HdzFcmEDfZD)
		- [[z7eq2nvgus5b1rs9cqt18g6|Effective system design requires insights drawn from serious contexts of use]]
		- Of course, YC’s lessons aren’t “lessons” in a traditional sense—they’re experienced as the natural consequence of your actions in that environment: YC is an [[z92tgmibsnraf5kxxstnkbj|Enacted experience]].
		- Even though you don’t know what you’re doing as a founder ([Novices in enabling environments often can’t do what’s enabled](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zPrYC99kH227c1otipAefyQ)), YC’s systems give you enough support to participate authentically and hone your skills in this intense context: [Enacted experiences can bootstrap active participation in enabling environments](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zKubRcJXKjiCDPsPgJWckkP)
		- But those experiences aren’t an accident: YC’s enacted lessons are carefully *authored*, in the same sense that a book is. [Enacted experiences can create intense personal connection to authored targets](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zJxSaJaCeXZsY7pH2Q6t89t); [Enacted experiences amplify the power of narrative](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zQ9BWTY2JK6eJvCSqupc7UF).
- Unfortunately, [Enacted experiences are hard to author](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z8r1EZL3sFrFe7brjiwFbFN) and [Enacted experiences are hard to distribute](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zGNVRh1q5gGeF88PbmYu4BB).
	- But there’s hope: [[z984zzlbg4wrem6hazaovmb|Enacted experiences have incredible potential as a mass medium]]
- Video game design is the best prior art we have for this.
	- (some core notes on games and enacted experiences in [[z92tgmibsnraf5kxxstnkbj|Enacted experience]]; needs to be refactored)
		- emotional experiences and narrative unfold as a consequence of player action
		- and they’re good at building skills: [Games effectively develop players’ skills](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z9VDvQzzC4JD4DBWMVaVGjk)
		- games demonstrate how dynamic media can do things that books can’t do: [Games help players evaluate their developing skills](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zJP88EJ3qnPdZLMFK9g4Boh) and [Games help players make and adapt plans](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/z8cCUawpidfMcH7bYE67CEf)
		- but games are an aesthetic medium; they’re about [“Find the fun”](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zHAGZzVW1jygpT2SoFXZwSA); they’re not externally-oriented like non-fiction and YC: [Skill development in games is subservient to other intrinsically meaningful purposes](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zKt9wffdgJVT97ca4roAUWD)
