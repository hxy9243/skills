## The need for a revolution

In 1862, Lev Tolstoy expressed his horror with the [Prussian Model of Education](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Prussian_Model_of_Education "Prussian Model of Education"). On the 160th anniversary of his opposition, it is time to realize that the **evolution of the school system is ineffective**. Lev tried to start his own evolutionary process with a short-lived effort to set up his own version of democratic schools.

100 years ago, the evolution towards freedom seems to have accelerated with [Summerhill](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Summerhill "Summerhill"). The process accelerated again in the US with the birth of [Sudbury Valley School](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sudbury_Valley_School "Sudbury Valley School") in the 1960s. [John Holt](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/John_Holt "John Holt") contributed to a thriving [homeschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeschooling "Homeschooling") movement in the US in the 1980s. [John Taylor Gatto](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/John_Taylor_Gatto "John Taylor Gatto") and [Peter Gray](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Peter_Gray "Peter Gray") had a major contribution in the unfolding of the [unschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Unschooling "Unschooling") philosophy. Today, roughly 1% of children in the US can say they engage in [free learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning") with no concerns, and with few directions from the adult world. In the meantime, before the start of school in Poland, nearly all kids look depressed. They universally hate schooling (see: [Why kids hate school?](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Why_kids_hate_school%3F "Why kids hate school)). If 160 years of evolution delivered nearly no tangible fruit to an average kid in the western world, how long can we hope to wait until the evolution makes kids happy and productive at school? This text will argue that the answer is: **never**!

The evolution of schooling proceeds too slowly to offer much hope

## Local minimum

In mathematical optimization, we often end up in a local minimum. In such cases we need a major breakthrough to avoid getting stuck with a wrong solution. [Compulsory school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_school "Compulsory school") cannot be subject to incremental improvement. It cannot evolve into a better system because it is based on coercion and the coercion is written into the law (see: [Education as a human right](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Education_as_a_human_right "Education as a human right")). If it takes 60-100 years in the west to change the law to make unschooling legal, we cannot bank on politicians introducing necessary changes. We need to force their hand with the rebellion. Compulsory schooling cannot be optimized as long as it is compulsory and coercive. It keeps optimizing in the wrong direction. It keep digging the hole of the local minimum deeper. Finland is presented as a good example of how schools can evolve. I see Finland as stuck in a local minimum (see: [Finnish paradox](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Finnish_paradox "Finnish paradox")).

Metaphorically, local minimum can be compared to dinosaurs that cannot evolve into humans because of being stuck in the evolutionary comfort zone. Humans needed some help from an asteroid.

A coal mine cannot be incrementally improved into a climate-friendly facility because as long as it serves its main purpose: mining a fossil fuel to be sold as fuel, it will remain harmful. It needs to be torn down.

Democracies tends to get stuck in local minima that have self-sustaining qualities. Those minima put civilization at peril. Today, the worst human error I can identify is [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling").

We cannot evolve out from [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling") because it is stuck in a self-sustaining local minimum

## Positive forces

Arguably, the acceleration of the dissemination of information via the web is a ray of hope. The strongest forces favoring progress come from the web technologies associated with self-learning and social media. However, we need to recall that [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling") is a self-sustaining phenomenon. It keeps indoctrinating new generations, who hate school while attending it, and who change their mind within 3-10 years since graduation. Alternatively, the indoctrination makes student never realize their bondage (see: [Glorification of schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Glorification_of_schooling "Glorification of schooling")). The transition from hate to appreciation of school is based on the deficit of [educational empathy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Educational_empathy "Educational empathy"), which is a natural phenomenon affecting everyone. To understand the harm of school, one needs to study school or the brain. It took me many years of study to see the light! The problem of schooling was all around for me to see, and I never truly understood it. My sin was freedom at school, freedom at home, and my projection from my own happy state to the state of other students. Things worked out for me, so I did not pause too long to think why they might not work for others. Those with a bit of success at or after school, keep spreading the wrong meme. My meme was *"many years of schooling may lead to pioneering innovative technologies"*. That meme is false and it is treacherous. Upon closer scrutiny, school was always an obstacle. If it helped in any way, it was by slowing others more, which gave me some competitive "advantage". In reality, we all lost as humanity, and it is time to stop losing!

On the web, we see easier access to information, and better educational materials and tools. In this context, [spaced repetition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition") is now taken for granted and nearly fades into the background. [Incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading") will soon feel like old news with a volley of alternative technologies playing similar or complementary roles (see: [Inevitability of incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Inevitability_of_incremental_reading "Inevitability of incremental reading")). However, even here the school system has its answers in promoting the notion that digital technologies are a threat. Parents are told to impose screentime limits and children (see: [The morbid myth of Digital Dementia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/The_morbid_myth_of_Digital_Dementia "The morbid myth of Digital Dementia")). In China, its the parents who asked the government to limit legal screentime for children. Their prime parental motivation is to grind children through the machine of [coercive learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_learning "Coercive learning"), which China perceives as the acme of hope for better future. Even the UK, which makes [unschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Unschooling "Unschooling") possible, was on the list of governments considering ways to limit screentime for children (see: [Screentime limits](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Screentime_limits "Screentime limits")).

Even if the web can compress the evolution of school from 50 to 5 years, it is still 5 years of wasted life multiplied by a billion of students

## Contrary forces

Among the advocates of the evolution, there is a factor that seems to be universally overlooked. There are forces that act to preserve the current coercive status quote. The school system is designed for self-preservation and for cranking up the performance as per its own erroneous optimization criteria. As the mainstream of schooling is optimized for tests and grades (incl. [PISA](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/PISA "PISA")), it tends to efficiently fend off attack from progressive forces, from reformers, from science, from common sense, and from the evolutionary forces that increase the chances for reform.

While great authors such as Illich, Holt and Gatto left a legacy of inspirational thought, they are all dead. In the meantime, the school system is churning out new [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") generations that have no idea those authors ever existed. A portion of the population drifts towards [homeschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeschooling "Homeschooling") or even [unschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Unschooling "Unschooling") (in those few countries were it is legal), but they keep being perceived as fringe. Homeschoolers are constantly subjected to social pressures, incl. from their own and within their own family to return to [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") standards.

Death disproportionately affects the wise. Birth and education favor the coercive system. If we look at the level of diagnosis in [ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/ADHD "ADHD"), [dyslexia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dyslexia "Dyslexia") or at the increase in suicide, we might conclude that things keep evolving towards the worse. Instead of improving, we are drifting backwards with more and more young lives stolen. The indoctrination into coercion is getting more efficient.

In addition to counter-evolutionary forces, there are stochastic swing that pop up unpredictably. [Summerhill](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Summerhill "Summerhill") 's [battles with Office for Standards in Education](http://www.summerhillschool.co.uk/summerhills-fight.php) became legendary (see [the movie](https://youtube.com/watch)). There are eager social engineers like Emmanuel Macron or Jan Björklund, who are ready to turn education upside down on a political whim. There are also counter-revolutionaries like [Martha Albertson Fineman](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Martha_Albertson_Fineman "Martha Albertson Fineman") or [Elizabeth Bartholet](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Elizabeth_Bartholet "Elizabeth Bartholet") who provide legal arguments for tightening intellectual slavery. All those people are social, legal, or political activists with negligible understanding of the brain and its needs in development.

Evolution of education is effectively blocked by the evolutionary and revolutionary forces of unfreedom combined with undemocratic discrimination of the youth

## Slavery

One of my young friends decided to quit our school strike organization (SUSSEN). He said: *"There is no need to destroy things. Let's focus on building"*. That statement comes from not understanding our mission: [Declaration of Educational Emancipation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Declaration_of_Educational_Emancipation "Declaration of Educational Emancipation"). Freedom is not destructive even though a change from bondage to freedom may have undesirable side effects. Freedom for the slaves entailed the bloodiest war in American history. It does not invalidate the fact that emancipations of the slaves was the only obvious and just course of action. If Lincoln or others had different knowledge, history could have taken a different course. It might be less bloody. The choice of emancipation was correct, and the horrible consequences were rooted in the evil status quo preceding the emancipation. When we discuss school strike, I hear a great deal of angry young voices that need to be tempered. I cannot control these emotions even at this early stage. The consequences are unpredictable, but the school strike seems like the only feasible route towards educational freedom.

An alternative for Lincoln or educational reformers might be "gradual freedom" or "freedom associated with a package of reforms". Slaves might be released gradually. Complex reforms might soften economic consequences of freeing slaves. However, a call for freedom and the respect for human rights is atomic, simple and hermetic. Adding complexities is likely to cause rifts that undermine the cause. Complex reforms can be presented in parallels, but will face the same obstacles as all reforms in democracies. The aim of the strike must be freedom. Freedom will necessitate reforms that will come in the wake at higher urgency.

## Democracy

Children cannot vote in a democracy. Nor could slaves in the US. Children and slaves are/were stuck in a system which they can/could not change. The right of children to determine their own future is violated by [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling"). They keep being indoctrinated while having no voice. Once they leave school, they are a perfect material for the maintenance of the status quo. This means that all forms of evolution may stagnate at a set level of population able to enjoy [free learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning") (assuming changes in the law). This is why the kids need to rebel at the time when they know best that their rights are being violated. See also: [Children should have the right to vote](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Children_should_have_the_right_to_vote "Children should have the right to vote")

One of the self-sustaining powers of the system is the cap on the ability of children to vote in elections and referenda

## Paradigm shift

Thomas Kuhn observed that paradigm shifts are abrupt and revolutionary. Evolution may favor stagnation or even paradigm paralysis when old models dominate the groupthink of " [normal science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Normal_science) ". A paradigm shift has already occurred in the minds of lead educators. Today, all people with rudimentary understanding of education know that coercion is counterproductive. It is the inertia of the coercive system that keeps it in check. We have built an elaborate system of checks and balances that form the shackles with no panic button at sight. We have shackled societies! Unlike climate change, unshackling should be easy. We need to simply ban coercion! This should quickly make coercion culturally unacceptable.

If children cannot see the effects of scientific paradigm shift, it is only due to the fact that we need one more paradigm shift: the shift in active opposition to the present system.

Paradigm shift on [coercion in learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercion_in_learning "Coercion in learning") has occurred. Now we need a paradigm shift on the need for a revolution

## Revolutionary wave

When circumstances are ripe, a revolution needs just a spark. Once a revolution is on a good course, it can inspire further change, and further revolutions that are called **revolutionary waves**. Arab Spring, Prague Spring, Croatian Spring, or the Spring of the Nations (1848) are good examples. Perhaps it is then time for **Student Spring**. If one country decided to free the students, no student in the world would rest easy until she had the same rights as the students in the lucky pioneering country. Perhaps all we need is not a country, but just a city, town or even a single school. For that just one Greta might start the dominos of the revolutionary wave that could free a billion students!

Prior waves came slowly and needed decades or centuries to mature. It was a similar story in case of the emancipation of slaves, women, serfs, and the like. In hindsight, violations of human rights seem clear in all those cases, while the school maintains an aura of a good institution that serves students and their enlightenment. We still largely believe children to be immature and incapable of controlling their own development. However, if we look at the perception of human rights from the perspectives prior to individual revolutions, social conviction was often equally weak as today's adult readiness to abolish the old school system. The end of serfdom had its roots in the Black Death epidemic. Perhaps, it is the coronavirus epidemic that will mark then end of student bondage.

The only thing we need to achieve in the first step is a declaration of any power figure that **coercion in education should be illegal**.
