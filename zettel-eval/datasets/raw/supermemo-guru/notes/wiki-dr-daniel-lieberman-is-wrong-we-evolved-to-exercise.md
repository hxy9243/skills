[Dr Daniel Lieberman: The evolution of laziness: Why humans resist the gym](https://youtube.com/watch)

## Inaccurate claim

Well-intended professor combats pernicious myths that poison our take on modern life, sleep and exercise. With the audience's interest in mind, he diminishes the negative influences of modern lifestyle, by contrasting it with lifestyle of hunter-gatherers. That's a goldmine of precious observations. However, one claim might not be too accurate, and possibly misleading. His message is deeply compassionate. He explains why 80% of Americans do not exercise and why we should not make them feel bad about it. Lieberman attributes this behavior to evolutionary adaptations that discourage expending unnecessary calories above the basal metabolic rate that still consumes two thirds of the total.

However, to promote a healthier and more accurate perspective, in this text I will prove the opposite: **we evolved to exercise**!

Even though I do have formal training in biochemistry, molecular biology, and human physiology, my proof is a bit pop scientific. I derive it from observations collected in my own lifestyle that gradually evolved away from the gifts of the civilization. However, I consider my reasoning hermetic, and I bet I might convince Prof. Lieberman himself!

## Hunter-gatherers

Lieberman says he cannot experiment on the evolution, so he seeks people in natural environments that hint at the ultimate outcomes of evolutionary mechanisms. He travels to Mexico, Kenya or Tanzania to observe ancient cultures untouched by modern influences. That's a unique and precious perspective. However, I also have my own experimental ground. Looking for a healthy lifestyle where my only monstrous interference is the connection to the world via the Internet. A lifestyle where each day seems to follow the same freely evolving pattern that is largely unaffected by the outside world. With four decades of observations of such a slow pattern evolution, I stake my claim on understanding the pull of natural forces. I am no hunter, I do not gather, I am influenced by modern culture, but I seem to evolve towards natural behaviors by removing all traditional obstacles and dogma.

## Natural laziness

Lieberman separates physical activity from exercise. Physical activity serves a practical purpose (e.g. shopping), while exercise goes beyond that and serves the purpose of health. Thus, exercise is physical activity for physical activity's sake. This raises the bar of evidence for my proof.

Lieberman argues that humans did not evolve to exercise for its own sake, but rather to move when necessary for survival: hunting, foraging, or escaping danger. Since food scarcity shaped our evolution, conserving energy whenever possible was advantageous, which is why people today often feel reluctant to work out. Physical activity in ancestral environments was always tied to immediate goals, not long-term health. In modern societies, where survival no longer demands daily exertion, this evolutionary bias creates a mismatch, leaving us with sedentary lifestyles and the need to "invent" exercise as a deliberate practice.

Lieberman claims we are naturally lazy. Exercise is a modern invention

## Exercise vs. learning

Reward works differently in learning and in exercise.

**In learning**, it is possible to receive continual rewards for progress in gathering knowledge, [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity") and [problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving"). Adaptation occurs via pleasurable enhancement of knowledge.

**In exercise**, adaptation requires exertion beyond the baseline. The brain prevents excessive exertion by issuing pain penalties. However, optimally incremental increase in exertion results in progress in adaptation. The reward is dished out after exercise and is enhanced by the measure of progress.

Learning is continually pleasurable. Adaptive exercise nearly always involves a degree of pain

While learning may progress throughout the life. Exercise mechanism seems to favor settling down on the equilibrium in which the adaptation goes only as far as necessary. However, the equilibrium depends on the magnitude of reward as we can always see major rewards through minor pain. What is the true natural equilibrium we evolved for?
