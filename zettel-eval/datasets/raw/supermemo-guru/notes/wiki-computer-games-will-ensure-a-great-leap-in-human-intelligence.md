At school we learn many myths about [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"). For example, schools claim falsely that they serve education and thus support intelligence. In reality, all coercive approaches to education hurt both learning and intelligence.

Another myth is that the intelligence depends on the quality of the "processor". By analogy to computers, we tend to believe falsely that those born with a fast processor are destined to be smarter.

In reality, maximization of intelligence is possible only relative to a select target environment. Neither school nor genes have much to say in that equation.

Maximization occurs within a given environment by unconstrained adaptation. Enhancement of adaptation and intelligence occurs via variability of the environment. There is an optimum degree of variability that maximizes adaptation. Stable environment maximizes adaptation to that environment in conditions that will change little in the future. Dynamic changes in the environment lead to a different kind of adaptation: less accurate, but more universal. Changes that are too fast may make adaptation ineffectual.

School is an artificial environment in which the student adapts to the environment of school. This adaptation is of little use in real life. If students don't get lost at graduation it is only because they spend sufficient time outside school in conditions of reasonable freedom. They do live a bit in the "real world". Adaptation to school leads to [100 bad habits learned at school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/100_bad_habits_learned_at_school "100 bad habits learned at school").

Maximization of intelligence is best left to a free brain operating under the guidance of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"). What educators can do is to affect the environment that may induce changes to the adaptation trajectory through voluntary choices of the brain.

A new fantastic opportunity in education arose in recent years: **computers games**. Most of attractive computer games provide a form of simulation of a fraction of the real world. This makes it possible to learn to fly without risking anybody's life. This makes possible to enjoy explosions or rocket flights. This opens the world of physics to explore, and the world of artificial intelligence to change human reasoning from [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") to the one with good intuitions about [conceptual computation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptual_computation "Conceptual computation").

With fast progress in artificial intelligence and virtual reality, children can now make their own adaptation choices. They can do easy, cheap, and free **sampling** of the opportunities of life. They can chart their own course to the future. They may wish to become Andre Stern or Elon Musk or Herbie Hancock or Isaac Newton or a pizza shop owner. On the way they can acquire their own toolset optimized for their own life.

We all know that coercive schools do not work. It is time to understand that [free learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning") is the future. [Unschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Unschooling "Unschooling") is the future. Computer games will make it easier.

Open minded parents and teachers need to assist in the transition to that new reality because the old world of entrenched coercive schooling is set on destroying the competition that comes from gaming.
