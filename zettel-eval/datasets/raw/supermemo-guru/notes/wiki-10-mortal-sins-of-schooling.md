This text is part of: " *[I would never send my kids to school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_of_Schooling "Problem of Schooling")* " by [Piotr Wozniak](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Piotr_Wozniak "Piotr Wozniak") (2017)

In [this book](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_of_schooling "Problem of schooling"), I list a few dozen **cardinal sins** of "modern" school systems. Here is just a quick summary.

The deadly sins of [passive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Passive_schooling "Passive schooling") and [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling"):

- schooling stifles the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), which results in inefficient learning
- schooling stifles [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity"), and leads to [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness")
- schooling stifles [problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving"), and results in weak [generalization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Generalization "Generalization") capacity
- schooling stifles exploratory learning (see: [Education counteracts evolution](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Education_counteracts_evolution "Education counteracts evolution"))
- schooling stifles [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") due suppressing choice, and requiring [asemantic learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Asemantic_learning "Asemantic learning")
- schooling stunts [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") by interfering with the [conceptualization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptualization "Conceptualization") process in development
- schooling fails to optimize for long-term [active recall](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Active_recall "Active recall") (e.g. as facilitated with [spaced repetition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition"))
- schooling undermines the [structure of knowledge](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Structure_of_knowledge "Structure of knowledge"), and comprehension
- schooling powers [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference") that favors [forgetting](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Forgetting "Forgetting") of the hurriedly [crammed](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Cramming "Cramming") knowledge (see: [On the superiority of a rat over a schooled human](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/On_the_superiority_of_a_rat_over_a_schooled_human "On the superiority of a rat over a schooled human"))
- schooling results in the hate of learning (see: [Why kids hate school?](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Why_kids_hate_school%3F "Why kids hate school))
- schooling conditions the belief that the displeasure in learning is natural (see: [Pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning"))
- schooling interferes with [sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep "Sleep"), the [circadian cycle](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian_cycle "Circadian cycle"), and the [natural creativity cycle](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Natural_creativity_cycle "Natural creativity cycle") (see: [Delayed Sleep Phase Syndrome (DSPS)](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Delayed_Sleep_Phase_Syndrome_\(DSPS\) "Delayed Sleep Phase Syndrome (DSPS)"))
- schooling results in [chronic stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Chronic_stress "Chronic stress")
- schooling undermines self-esteem and self-discipline (e.g. by [labelling kids lazy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Myth:_students_are_lazy "Myth: students are lazy"))
- schooling suppresses the need and love of exercise
- schooling affects mental health: [bullying](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Bullying "Bullying"), school refusal, [ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/ADHD "ADHD"), depression, suicide, as well as [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory") with ramifications for [dyslexia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dyslexia "Dyslexia"), [dyscalculia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dyscalculia), [dysgraphia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dysgraphia), etc.
- schooling affects health in general (e.g. obesity, diabetes, [eyesight](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Harm_of_eyeglasses "Harm of eyeglasses"), etc.)
- schooling establishes a pathological social environment based on a [closed systems of socialization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Closed_systems_of_socialization "Closed systems of socialization") in age segregated groups (this results, for example, in [bullying](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Bullying "Bullying"))
- schooling undermines [knowledge diversity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/On_freedom_of_education_and_freedom_of_information "On freedom of education and freedom of information") in society
- schooling is used for the [indoctrination of children](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_curriculum_is_inherently_political "School curriculum is inherently political")
- schools promote dozens of harmful myths about learning (see: [Mythology of schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Mythology_of_schooling "Mythology of schooling"))
- school instills dozens of bad habits that often last for life (see: [100 bad habits learned at school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/100_bad_habits_learned_at_school "100 bad habits learned at school"))
- **schools steal the best years of youth**

[Compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling") is a violation of human rights

In conclusion, [compulsory schooling must end](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling_must_end "Compulsory schooling must end"). It should be replaced with [free learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning"). Survival of the present system of education is rooted in rich [school mythology](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Mythology_that_keeps_the_archaic_school_system_alive "Mythology that keeps the archaic school system alive") that needs to be abolished before change is possible. For an alternative vision of the future see: [Grand Education Reform](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Grand_Education_Reform "Grand Education Reform"). See also: [Declaration of Educational Emancipation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Declaration_of_Educational_Emancipation "Declaration of Educational Emancipation")

Learn drive vs. School drive

> ***Figure:** This is how school destroys the love of learning. **[Learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")** is the set of passions and interests that a child would like to pursue. **[School drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_drive "School drive")** is the set of rewards and penalties set up by the school system. **Learn drive** leads to simple, mnemonic, [coherent](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherent "Coherent"), [stable](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stable "Stable") and [applicable](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Applicable "Applicable") memories due to the fact that the quality of knowledge determines the degree of reward in the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") system. **School drive** leads to complex, short-term memories vulnerable to [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference") due to the fact that schools serialize knowledge by [curriculum](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curriculum "Curriculum") (not by the neural mechanism of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")). [Competitive inhibition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/War_of_the_networks "War of the networks") between the **Learn drive** and the **School drive** circuits will lead to the weakening of neural connections. Strong **School drive** will weaken the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), destroy the passion for learning, and lead to [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"). Powerful **Learn drive** will lead to [rebellion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") that will protect intrinsic passions, but possibly will also lead to problems at school. Storing new knowledge under the influence of **Learn drive** is highly rewarding and carries no penalty (by definition of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")). This will make the learn drive thrive leading to success in learning (and at school). In contrast, poor quality of knowledge induced by the pressures of the **School drive** will produce a weaker reward signal, and possibly a strong [incoherence penalty](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incoherence_penalty "Incoherence penalty"). The penalty will feed back to produce [reactance](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") against the [school drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_drive "School drive"), which will in turn require further coercive correction from the school system, which will in turn reduce the quality of knowledge further. Those feedback loops may lead to the dominance of one of the forces: the learn drive or the school drive. Thriving [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") increases [rebellion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") that increases defenses against the school drive. Similarly, increased penalization at school increases [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") that weakens the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") and results in submission to the system. Sadly, in most cases, the control system settles in the middle of those two extremes (see: the [old soup problem](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Old_soup_problem "Old soup problem")). Most children [hate school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Why_kids_hate_school%3F "Why kids hate school), lose their love of learning, and still submit to the enslavement. Their best chance for recovery is the freedom of college, or better yet, the freedom of adulthood. See: [Competitive feedback loops in binary decision making at neuronal level](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Competitive_feedback_loops_in_binary_decision_making_at_neuronal_level "Competitive feedback loops in binary decision making at neuronal level")*
