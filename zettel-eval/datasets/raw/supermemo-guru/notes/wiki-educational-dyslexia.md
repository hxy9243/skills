## Definition

**Educational dyslexia** is reading difficulty induced by the stress of schooling or rigorous instruction. The terms **school dyslexia** or **induced dyslexia** may also be used in other sources.

## Mechanism

The mechanism of **educational dyslexia** (or school-induced dyslexia) is the same as the formation of [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory") in all forms of [coercive learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_learning "Coercive learning"). When a child learns to associate reading with displeasure, the learning process may grind to a halt. A regress in reading skills is also possible. Sensitive children with passions that do not involve reading are at particular risk. **Educational dyslexia** is most likely to arise in early reading instruction when the brain has not reached reading readiness, which may in part be architectural and in part associated with the necessary [knowledge scaffolding](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Semantic_framework "Semantic framework").

The outline of the mechanism of **educational dyslexia**:

- optimal learning is guided by the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") (see: [Optimality of the learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Optimality_of_the_learn_drive "Optimality of the learn drive"))
- all pieces of knowledge are fit like jigsaw puzzle, optimally (see: [Jigsaw puzzle metaphor](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Jigsaw_puzzle_metaphor "Jigsaw puzzle metaphor"))
- the critical part of that puzzle is [valuation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Valuation "Valuation") (see: [knowledge valuation network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation_network "Knowledge valuation network")) and [semantic distance](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Semantic_distance "Semantic distance") (i.e. the length of the semantic pathways the brain needs to travers to connect pieces of knowledge)
- an easy and reliable measure of the optimality of learning is the [pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning")
- an [unschooler](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning") builds a jigsaw puzzle of reading knowledge
- to master reading, this gradual buildup of knowledge takes a long time because there are thousands of pieces of knowledge to connect
- when reading happens seemingly overnight, it is a result of long months of implicit buildup of knowledge that occurs each time a child is in contact with text
- at school, the [semantic distance](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Semantic_distance "Semantic distance") and the [knowledge valuation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation") are ignored. Students are supposed to do what they are told, and they are told to master skills as prescribed in the [curriculum](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curriculum "Curriculum") or a specific textbook
- if the brain cannot make a connection between pieces of knowledge (i.e. it cannot traverse the [semantic distance](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Semantic_distance "Semantic distance")) there is stress involved (see: [decoding failure penalty](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Decoding_failure_penalty "Decoding failure penalty"))
- if the connection fails, the input (i.e. a question, a word, a syllable, etc.), instead of being connected with he right output (e.g. sound, meaning, etc.), will be associated with stress
- if the reading procedure evokes a great deal of wrong associations (called [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory")), it will lead to anxiety, and a dislike of reading (or even fear of reading instruction)
- the state of anxiety makes learning hardly possible. Outwardly, the inability to make progress in simplest reading tasks will manifest as dyslexia
- if reading activates a great deal of [toxic memories](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memories "Toxic memories"), defense mechanisms may be triggered (e.g. avoidance of reading, school, friends, etc.). Further learning becomes impossible

See also: [Schools contribute to the dyslexia epidemic](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schools_contribute_to_the_dyslexia_epidemic "Schools contribute to the dyslexia epidemic") and [Dyslexia Conspiracy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dyslexia_Conspiracy "Dyslexia Conspiracy")

## Remedy

An instant remedy to the problem of **educational dyslexia** is to remove the pressure of schooling. When a child is transferred to an environment in which there is no pressure to read, the school dyslexia will likely stop its progression (see: [Gray: Mechanics of educational dyslexia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Gray:_Mechanics_of_educational_dyslexia "Gray: Mechanics of educational dyslexia")). However, the process of learning may still be long and requiring a great deal of patience. In the first step, the child needs to unlearn all [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory"). This can be very difficult if the period of toxic learning spanned many years. Some children may be marked for life, and reading will never be as fluent as in other adults. However, if the dyslexia was noticed early in the process of schooling (e.g. the first 1-2 years), the process of unlearning may take just a few months. [Unschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Unschooling "Unschooling") by definition provides no pressure of progress. Even if the child refuses to take a book into his hand, the inaction should be interpreted as a freedom from toxic stimuli that reinforce [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory"). As Gray noticed, if there is no neurological basis of the problem, the child will learn to read sooner or later. It may be one-on-one time with mom, or fun time with peers, or even interactive computer games that involve sufficient exposure to text. There is no good research to back my wild guess, but it is conceivable that a majority of cases of dyslexia at school is **educational dyslexia** that can resolve spontaneously given sufficient freedom and rich exposure to text with no pressure to learn. Analogies to the problem of [ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/ADHD "ADHD") are plenty. Many dyslexia expert would be horrified with my advice. Their mantra is "diagnose early, and remedy expertly". All I know about [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory") tells me that freedom might be a safer choice in a healthy child. A few months of observation should suffice to see signs of progress or increased interest.

## Origins

The term **school dyslexia** was probably first used by Samuel Blumenfeld (see: [Reading wars](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Reading_wars_are_over:_Whole_language_vs._Phonics "Reading wars are over: Whole language vs. Phonics"), [Accuracy in reading is costly](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Accuracy_in_reading_is_costly "Accuracy in reading is costly")).

> Are there people who are born dyslexic? Yes, but they are afflicted with so many other problems that their inability to learn to read is simply only one of them. There are children born with all sorts of handicaps and defects that are recognized at birth or soon after. Some of these handicaps reflect neurological problems. But many of these children are quite educable. However, the dyslexia we are talking about is the kind that afflicts children who have come to school with perfectly good speech, hearing, eyesight, equilibrium, etc. In fact, some of these so-called dyslexics are some of the brightest and physically healthiest students in their classes. Miller calls their reading problem "educational dyslexia," that is, dyslexia, or reading disability, caused by the teaching method

[Danny Greenberg](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Danny_Greenberg "Danny Greenberg") noted no cases of dyslexia in [Sudbury Valley School](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sudbury_Valley_School "Sudbury Valley School") in half a century of school's existence. This inspired Je'anna L Clements to research the topic and write [What If School Creates DYSlexia?](https://www.smashwords.com/books/view/1048359). My own reasoning about **educational dyslexia** stems from my interest in [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory"), which itself can be studied in [spaced repetition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition"). In [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo"), [leeches](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Leech "Leech") can transform into [toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory") over time. If reading was to be studied in SuperMemo, we could quickly arrive to a buildup of toxic memories that would inevitably cause reading anxiety. The best way to learn reading is an interactive exposure to text (e.g. self-learning with computer games, or interactive learning with a peer, parents, or a friend). The best pathway to reading fluency is reading itself.

## Neurological origins

It is not a great surprise that students affected by **educational dyslexia** show different characteristics in brain scans (see: [Dyslexia conspiracy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dyslexia_conspiracy "Dyslexia conspiracy")). We know that training and prolific use of cortical areas results in anatomical changes that can easily be demonstrated with modern brain imaging. However, in all cases of the correlation between structural changes and functionality, we need to ponder causality. Neurological injury will obviously affect skills and behavior. At the same, learning deficits will be reflected in structure deficits. The development of gray and white matter structures involved in reading proceeds in parallel with the development of reading skills. The definition of **educational dyslexia** excludes neurological origin, however, neurological deficits may contribute to developing **educational dyslexia** as all forms of retardation are likely to increase the stress of schooling.

In addition, studies of [white matter development](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dual-process_model_of_white-matter_development "Dual-process model of white-matter development") indicate that some forms of wiring may underlie difficulty in learning to read. The usual interpretation is that "bad wiring" might indicate neurodevelopmental nature of dyslexia. This intensifies calls for early intervention. However, white matter remolding may be observed in brain scans after just weeks of intense learning. For this reason, we need to keep in mind an alternative interpretation. Persistent educational dyslexia may be a result of suboptimal wiring due to coercive imposition of wrong learning strategies. Once wrong tracks are laid, it may be hard for the brain to discover optimum wiring ([toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory") is characterized by its pernicious persistence).

### Atomic toxic memory

![](https://supermemo.guru/images/thumb/7/7d/Toxic_memory_diagram.png/500px-Toxic_memory_diagram.png)

> ***Figure:** **[Toxic memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memory "Toxic memory")** forms when learning penalties interfere with learning rewards and make it impossible to resolve conflicts in an interference zone of a pattern recognition network. The diagram shows a simple [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") illustrating the process of forming [toxic memories](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Toxic_memories "Toxic memories"). In the diagram, the brain attempts to associate a visual pattern of a letter " **Y** " with its correct pronunciation* \[waɪ\]*. In the course of learning, natural [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference") is a norm, and is quickly resolved by rewards of correct association signal on the output, and penalties of incorrect associations. If interference makes it impossible to establish a pattern in memory, the association is rejected and awaits subsequent opportunities to be re-established in a new configuration. However, in conditions of stress, the interference from penalties on output may prevent the resolution of conflicts in the interference zone. If the situation persists, e.g. as a result of frequent drills at school, [forgetting](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Forgetting "Forgetting") will not be able to clean up the unwanted memory. The interfering pattern may persist. Instead of enhancing synaptic connections in the pattern recognition network, it may enhance negative associations leading to intrinsic penalties (stemming from dissatisfaction with learning), and extrinsic penalties (e.g. dissatisfaction of a teacher, peer ridicule, etc.). The loop of extrinsic penalties reinforcing the interfering pattern may be particularly harmful. This is how [coercive instruction](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercion_in_learning "Coercion in learning") does harm to mental health of children. At later stages of this process, the pattern on input, instead of being resolved with the right association, generates an instant penalty signal. This occurs in educational dyslexia where all things associated with print and reading may bring up the state of anxiety or fear*
