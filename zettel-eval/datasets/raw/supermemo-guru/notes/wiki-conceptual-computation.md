## Definition

**Conceptual computation** is a computation that occurs in a [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network"). **Conceptual computation** makes up the key ingredient of human [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") (and future [artificial intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence")).

Reasoning, inference, or thinking are used colloquially to refer to the outwardly observable effects of **conceptual computation**.

There are three ingredients of human [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"):

- [Concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") makes the mind's hardware with two essential attributes (1) network-level reward needed for self-organization, and (2) function-dependent architecture
- [Memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neurostatistical_model_of_memory "Neurostatistical model of memory") makes the software of the mind that is largely based on micro-structural changes in the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network")
- **Conceptual computation** is the way mental software is run in mental hardware by means of (1) [spreading activation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spreading_activation "Spreading activation") and (2) attention

## Reasoning as computation

In [problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving"), it is helpful to see reasoning as a **conceptual computation**. It makes it possible to explain virtually all properties of the brain across multiple areas of study.

There are many models of computation. A [Turing Machine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_machine) is a classic example. Hereby, I present a simpler model that reflects of how the brain works. The model is illustrative and requires no formal mathematical language. It helps explain the operations of the brain in [problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving"). It is pretty trivial to show how a [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") might implement a Turing machine, with the extension of the brain through human culture approximating the infinite tape of the abstract machine. An important implication of this is that the human brain is virtually limitless in its capacity to model reality.

## Concept network

A **[concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network")** is a network of **[concepts](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept")** connected by **concept links**. In the human brain, the network corresponds with the ideas that can live in the mind.

**[Concept](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept")**: when the brain thinks of an object or an idea, a concept will light up in the mind. A concept corresponds with a set of brain cells that are activated upon the recognition of or whilst thinking about the object or idea. When thinking about a forest, a forest concept in the brain gets activated. At the very lowest neural level, a concept may, in theory, be represented by a [single neuron](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Grandmother_cell "Grandmother cell").

**Connection** (or **Concept link**): two concepts can be connected by a link. In the brain, it is a simple connection between cells. The concept of a forest is linked with the concept of a tree because forests are made of trees. The concept of a tree is linked with the concept of a branch. When thinking of a tree, there is an increase in the probability of thinking about the forest because the two form a concept link.

**[Concept map](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map")** is a set of concepts connected by **connections**. While thinking about having a nice time in a forest, the brain lights up a concept map that might include concepts such as the forest, self, pleasure, and perhaps other associated concepts like: blue sky, warmth, butterflies, bird songs, etc. A **concept map** represents the current status of the thoughts. A good time in a forest may feel complex and complete, full of details and experiences. However, in reality, it is just an amazingly small number of **concept maps** lighting up in the brain. Our minds convert map simplicity into a perceived complexity of experience.

**[Concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network")** is the vast network of [concepts](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept") stored in the brain. Many of those concepts are inter-connected. The whole operation of the brain is based on serial or parallel activation of concepts in the brain.

For details see: [Concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network")

## Attention

The brain is a parallel machine, i.e. it can do many things at the same time, and can activate many [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") at the same time. However, in the thought process, parallel activations are focused into a single window of attention. Whilst thinking, you follow a specific reasoning pathway that rarely branches out to start two or more parallel active computations. In general, it is not possible to seed your brain with a couple of problems to solve and work on them consciously in parallel. It is possible to switch, to multitask, but this is not true parallel computing. Two separate problems to solve may interfere with each other due to the need to reuse overlapping [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") that tend to form unique representations in the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network"). Thinking is pretty costly in terms of energy use. It is therefore possible that parallel computation would also lead to [homeostatic](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeostatic "Homeostatic") network fatigue. However, a great deal of parallel processing happens during sleep, and energy is less of a factor then. Most importantly though, the brain evolved as a central command organ. Its purpose is not to solve dozens of independent problems based on **conceptual computation**. Its main purpose is to decide the next step for the animal to take. For that sake, the brain collects available data from the senses, integrates it, and executes a plan. For the brain, the main goal is to find the next step in the currently executed plan. Parallel processes will be autonomous and/or subservient. Parallel processing may convert visual signals to a [concept map](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") representing an apple, its location, and physical availability. This translates to a concept *"food within reach"*, which helps execute the next step for a hungry animal: reaching for food. The same animal might have had some reproductive plan on its mind, but the brain will always go for a specific executive action. The reproductive plan and the corresponding [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") may remain in the state of high activation, but it won't be part of conscious processing, unless on some multitasking principle. In all likelihood, the well activated map will make the animal return to the reproductive plan once the apple will have been reached.

An **active map** is any [concept map](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") in the state of higher activation. It is sending signals to other portions of the brain. There may be dozens or hundreds of maps active at the same time. However, very few of those maps will affect conscious experience. Only one map will play a truly central role: the **focus map**.

The **focus map** is an active [concept map](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") that has a direct access to the chief executive. That map represents what you are currently thinking about. It is also the core map that determines the plan of action. The **focus map** determines what we do next.

## Spreading activation

A **brain state** is a set of active concept maps with one **focus map** in the center of attention.

A **brain state transition** is a change of the **focus map**, or the activation of new [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") along the rules of [spreading of activation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spreading_of_activation "Spreading of activation") with gradual fading of all activations left behind.

In **conceptual computation** (i.e. thinking), the brain undergoes a sequence of **brain state transitions** along the rules that determine concept map activation, fading, and memory.

**[Creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity")** is a form of stochastic search that probes [concept map](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") activations in order to meet a set of conditions, e.g. needed to construct an abstract [model](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Model "Model").

A solution in problem solving is a sequence of binary **concept associations**, which are formed by deductive reasoning (e.g. based on [fast thinking](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Fast_thinking "Fast thinking")), or by creative search.

**Concept association** (aka concept connection or concept link) is a new link between active concept maps. It corresponds with a new idea. An association forms a new concept link that may be used in the future. New links will affect the computation. All links are subject to valuation, and valuable links are strengthened. Future brain transitions are more likely to follow links that obtained high valuations. New associations leave a trail of progression towards the solution.

A **breakthrough association** is a new link established between concept maps. This link is an equivalent of a piece of knowledge that determines the solution. When a **breakthrough association** is formed in the brain, we experience the "aha moment", which is highly pleasurable. [Problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving") is a highly rewarding activity.

**Conceptual computation** (reasoning) is a simple chain of connected concepts streaming through the mind

## Problem solving

The thinking process is nothing else but a computation. We move between different brain states. Our conscious planning of the computation is just an illusion. In reality, the computation is the expression of [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") we wake up with, our general goals that are represented by maps that easily light up, and sub-goals that might be even easier to activate. On waking, a great morning idea may pop up first in the brain, then, unpredictably, most excitable active maps will light up. This first morning map may have a form of *"my usual morning routine"*, *"my usual basic goal(s)"*, *"my main worry"*, *"my key problem to solve"*, etc. Then there are things that come up because of news on TV, or messages on the phone, or aches and pains in the body, or worries, which have a form of easily activated maps that can invade the mind at any time.

Let's call the concept map associated with the problem to solve a **problem map**. The first thing we can do for long-term problem solving is to keep the problem map active. If this is the first map on waking, this is a first good step towards a solution! The problem map needs to be like a predator ready to snatch any inspiration and mold itself upon the influence.

Even the most complex problems can be reduced to simple links between simple ideas

Over the next 40 years, my work focused on efficient learning and memory, which are helpful in understanding the evolution of the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") in development, and in the course of lifelong learning.

For my own thinking, one of the most influential aspects of my work is [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading"). In incremental reading, I can see the structure of the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") unfold in front of my eyes. The two most striking components of that network are the transparency of its structure (as observable in [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo")), and its permanence (i.e. perpetuation of memory with [spaced repetition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition")). This means that it is possible to study the conceptual structure of memory in its physical representation (knowledge structure in the [collection](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Collection "Collection")) and its conceptual representation (concept network in the mind). This provides a unique insight into conceptual computation and all its coveted aspects such as attention, creativity, problem solving, etc.

In recent years, the stunning progress in understanding micro-histology of the brain added one final ingredient needed to understand the conceptual computation and how it sculpts the neuroarchitecture of the brain's [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network").

All evidence from all related branches of science makes it pretty clear. The future of [artificial intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence") will be based on the **conceptual computation**. The next step is the semantic web and an implementation of simple conceptual computation on the web that will link and integrate all of human intelligence into a powerful reasoning organism.
