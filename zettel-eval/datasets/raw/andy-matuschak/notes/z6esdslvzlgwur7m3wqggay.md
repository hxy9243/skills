[The mnemonic medium is particularly valuable for platform knowledge](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zLgARWxc2xbGQP7LC3s1CSe), but we suspect it can help readers connect more deeply to material in other genres, like:

- persuasive writing
- informal discussion
- reference material
	- Max Roser, 2020-06-15, points out that in some ways, OWiD is more like an augmented spreadsheet than like augmented prose.
		- [Cell Biology by the Numbers](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zNpHay3Eyohdk2mnjHvqNPC) is in between in an interesting way… it’s also like an augmented spreadsheet, but the expository prose adds much richness, akin to [Mnemonic medium prompts rely on invoking external experiences (from narrative, from real-world experience)](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zAwYPQGGGv2rPXVWRuHNgij)
- academic papers
- Twitter threads: [Twitter threads could be an interesting context for the mnemonic medium](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zJkR7LEzpMF7NzxuGZb1zwC)

Some challenges from work so far: [[zg29usta2l9lljkvbnvczcv|Lessons learned from applying the mnemonic medium to non-technical texts]]

One significant difference between these genres and platform knowledge will be significantly increased user variation—both in terms of motivation/purpose and in terms of prior knowledge. I expect this will require the medium to become more flexible. [[z3bazmung7hvamfhkytucwf|How might the mnemonic medium adapt to readers’ differing backgrounds and goals?]]

I suspect that one key approach will be using the medium less to build detailed memory and more in terms of [[zcquidgcnqncwb6ptvhdrwk|The mnemonic medium keeps readers in contact with material over time]].

One framing that bears on this question: [[zsktbk3mzrcu4dv9nfurqs7|What if the mnemonic medium were reframed as a means to engage more deeply with a text?]]
