## Introduction

In the US, the annual cost of common cold is $40 billion per year and this accounts for **[40% of all work lost](http://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamainternalmedicine/fullarticle/215118)**. My own productivity was also severely affected by infections decades ago, and this made me come up with a formula for common cold prevention. This formula worked magic for me. Perhaps my formula will inspire your own ways of cutting down on costs of infection.

## Origins of the formula

I am a productivity freak. I analyze and re-analyze productivity to identify weak spots. As early as 3 decades ago I identified **colds and influenza as the worst offenders** in lost productivity in my case. Today, I can say that I have managed to eliminate the problem almost entirely. Even though my work focuses on [sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Science_of_sleep "Science of sleep") and memory, the success of my "immunity workout" effort became remarkable enough to necessitate a short text, in which I should share my experience with others. I hold a degree in biology, which might have helped in designing a prevention program.

I charted my own infections from the year 1980. My worst year was 1995. In 1995, I had two major [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo") projects on my head, and also worked over my [PhD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Economics_of_learning "Economics of learning"). This was a year of stress and chaos. Naturally that resulted in reduced immunity. That single year, I had 7 cases of cold that resulted in 31 days of reduced productivity. The fact that I did not bother to take breaks from work must have had its contribution as well.

In the year 2000, there was a major drop in infections and the problem of lost productivity disappeared entirely by 2003. There are only 3 factors that could have produced those dramatic shifts. In the year 1999, I dropped all forms of [sleep regulation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_running_sleep "Free running sleep"), esp. the [alarm clock](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Alarm_clock "Alarm clock"). In the year 2003, I added weekly winter swimming and weekly half-marathon in the woods. No other solid explanations come to my mind.

If you ever hear a myth that innate immunity cannot be boosted because it is innate, review my case again. I would have somehow had to magically complete my adaptive immunity development around 2003.

## The anti-flu formula

Total freedom from infections is not possible, but a strong immune system will help fight all forms of infection and even act in prevention of other diseases, incl. cancer.

My formula for a clean bill of health:

- winter swimming every single week, no exceptions
- half marathon in the woods every single week, no exceptions (update 2020: I changed my sports schedule to be more evenly spread in a week)
- [free running sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_running_sleep "Free running sleep"), no exceptions
- avoiding exposure to people with fully blown symptoms of infection
- maximizing exposure to natural environment (outdoor sports, 1-3 hours daily, no exceptions)
- healthy diet and lifestyle (see: [Optimal diet](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Optimal_diet "Optimal diet"))
- stress management (I [love my work](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning"))

Other things that I do and other people tend not to do:

- solid rehydration. I am still amazed that people with significant interest in health do not know the value of profuse rehydration
- large daily dose of fresh vegetables
- sleeping with opened windows independent of the weather. The temperature in my room can drop down to 10 degrees Celsius on the coldest days of winter
- no use of home heating (also for ecological reasons)
- (update 2020) I touch my face only at home
- (update 2020) I do not travel (last 10 km trip by car: 2011)
- (update 2025) I avoid all human contact during epidemic waves of flu or covid (except for sports outdoors)

Other possibly contributing factors that have been introduced later:

- living life barefoot on a paved floor all day long, no exceptions (update 2023: my new record is 8 months without footware, from Apr 1, 2023 till Dec 1, 2023)
- exclusive use of shorts outdoors independent of the weather, all year long, no exceptions
- in winter swimming, I never miss full immersion of the head, which seems vital for clearing up the mucosa
- around 2017, inspired by the concept of the [war of the networks](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/War_of_the_networks "War of the networks"), I scaled down my attempts to keep my weight down (see: [Optimal diet](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Optimal_diet "Optimal diet"))

Literature on winter swimming is rich. It goes beyond the scope of this article. It all revolves around thermogenesis, mucosal protection, and the innate immune system. In summer, I try to swim outdoors daily, but it is that winter season that seems most important. It is possible that my sleep and work with open windows in low temperatures have a significant contribution as well. As I use no heating, I close windows only when the use of the keyboard becomes difficult due to low temperatures.

No innate immunity can prevent an infection in exposure to a large dose of a novel pathogen. Visiting a friend with a fully blown flu is a bad idea. I never do it.

I love the woods. I love jogging in rain. Woods in the rain provide a nice natural low-dose exposure to a nice deal of bugs that keep your innate immune system busy. This is my immune system workout.

I introduced my system in stages. I have totally [freed my sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_running_sleep "Free running sleep") in 1999, but it was not a hermetic formula against infections. Only in 2003, I have introduced a strict regimen with one half-marathon per week and one winter swimming session per week. I have kept it since. It is costly in terms of time, but it seems to pay back nicely in terms of health and productivity.

Winter swimming alone does not seem to be a solution either. I tried it many times in the 1980s and in the 1990s, but the missing ingredient must have been my limited respect for sleep (e.g. frequent all nighters during my college years).

## Success rate

I realize that my system is very hard to implement. For most people, it is impossible to implement due to time constraints. However, I needed to describe it as inspiration. Half-implementation is better than no implementation. It also attests to the power of workouts for the innate immune system. It is a proof that we can do a lot to boost our immunity. Immune system workout is not a myth!

None of my friends inspired by my program could entirely escape infections. In each case, I could identify main culprits as:

- high stress
- insufficient sleep
- insufficient prevention (e.g. when in contact with infected people)

As in other walks of life, self-discipline seems vital. Nobody in my group of friends seemed to have been able to reproduce my weekly regimen of winter swimming and half-marathons. It is hard for a season, let alone for a decade. However, winter swimmers regularly claim increased immunity (some claim to have not suffered any respiratory infections for decades). In the winter swimming community, the prevention of infections is one of the main reasons people continue the habit of getting exposed to cold water in winters.

## Coronavirus update (2020)

2019 coronavirus outbreak made me realize that I failed to list at least two habits that might be helpful in preventing infections in my own case. I do not travel and I do not touch my face. My prescriptive failure proves that it is hard to provide a hermetic formula to replicate a result.

I do not travel because I am very stingy about time and productivity. It might be hard to prevent infections in a small train compartment while travelling with a sneezer. Even more, I spend roughly 96-98% of my time in my room or outdoors. My time indoors is largely limited to places such a small deserted fish bar, an empty pub, or a small shop. I boasted of a thousand interviews with children for my *" [Problem of schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_of_schooling "Problem of schooling") "*. It is interesting to note that most of those interviews happened outdoors (e.g. on a football field, school playground, beach, etc.).

As for touching my face, I have been conditioned for decades not to do it. Perhaps it dates back to my learning microbiology in the early 1980s? Only a recent coronavirus emphasis on touching the face made me realize how strict I am. It also demonstrates the curse of knowledge. A single case of rubbing my eyes after touching a public door handle might ruin my formula, which I failed to describe in my original text (2017). I would literally never touch my eyes without touching soup and warm water first. This habit may date back even to my childhood. I simply always had very sensitive eyes. Many hours in front of the computer screen make me particularly scrupulous in avoiding any form of irritation.

Perhaps there are more habits that are helpful and I failed to notice? I should then add that good knowledge of health and microbiology is also a form of prevention.

## Coronavirus update (2022)

I never got vaccinated for covid, in part due to my scientific curiosity. I wanted to know more about my preventive formula. After the pandemic, my antibody test showed no sign of a past infection with covid (Dec 17, 2022). Either my meticulous avoidance of infection was successful, or my innate immunity helped me prevent the virus to enter the system. Most likely both. I never inhaled a dose that could overwhelm the system, and most of my exposure came at times of least risk (e.g. after swimming).

## Flu epidemic update (2025)

As my 12-year-old friend suffers in bed coughing. I mentioned my freedom from infections. His whole family was brought down during the flu wave we have in Poland these days. His reaction was *don't flex it on me*. It made me realize I must add an important observation: **Freedom from infection does not mean full freedom**. Not only do I want to avoid an infection, but I also think of my nice 22-year healthy streak. As I hear of more and more of my friends going down, I avoid meeting people. Even on a football field I avoid usual hugs and handshakes. Freedom from infection imposes other restrictions. They are not too burdensome though

## Summary

It is possible to avoid infections for decades in otherwise vulnerable individuals

Here are the most important things to remember:

- [good sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Science_of_sleep "Science of sleep") is essential for immunity
- [alarm clocks](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Alarm_clock "Alarm clock") destroy the quality of sleep
- [cold exposure is great for preventing colds](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Myth:_We_catch_a_cold_from_cold "Myth: We catch a cold from cold")
- winter swimming is one of the most effective forms of cold exposure
- moderate exercise helps prevent infections
- [stress management](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_resilience "Stress resilience") is essential for immunity
- profuse rehydration helps combat infections
- healthy diet and lifestyle help combat infections
- minor [exposure](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Daycare_infections "Daycare infections") to natural allergens and pathogens might be helpful (e.g. walk in a park on a rainy day)
- it is important to avoid heavy exposure to viruses during flu epidemics
- it is helpful to learn not to touch one's face
