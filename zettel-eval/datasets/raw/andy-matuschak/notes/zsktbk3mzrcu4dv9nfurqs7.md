The [[zkpv6qkserdrgqyryvgs2ws|Mnemonic medium]] encodes—in its name, even—its focus on helping readers build detailed memory of what they read. This is clearly quite helpful for platform knowledge ([The mnemonic medium is particularly valuable for platform knowledge](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zLgARWxc2xbGQP7LC3s1CSe)), but detailed memory usually isn’t the primary goal for other genres of writing.

In thinking about [[z6esdslvzlgwur7m3wqggay|How might the mnemonic medium enable readers in genres outside platform knowledge?]], one approach is to use the prompts less as a means for building detailed memory and more as a means for prompting deeper engagement (a la [[z5aepnacuu6xipqb4lgbdkh|The mnemonic medium may push readers to read more slowly and attentively]]), not just during the reading experience but stretching through time ([[zcquidgcnqncwb6ptvhdrwk|The mnemonic medium keeps readers in contact with material over time]]).

A related potential mechanism: [Embedded prompt templates may actively scaffold prompt-writing for mnemonic medium readers](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/zGZAVcDnNSnWF3oPLn2JmQp)
