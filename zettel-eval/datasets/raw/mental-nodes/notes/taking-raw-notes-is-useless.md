Mechanical note-taking (listening and passively writing down) is useless for memory and creativity.

First, the generation effect shows that we remember information better when [[keep-your-levels-of-consumption-and-creation-balanced|we create our own version]] of it.

Second, creativity requires the ability to connect the dots between many ideas and content sources, sometimes in surprising ways. Raw notes, [[folders-kill-creativity|especially when classified by topic or category or source]] and thus living in silos, do not allow for discovering these surprising connections. [[do-not-keep-orphan-notes|Do not keep orphan notes]].
