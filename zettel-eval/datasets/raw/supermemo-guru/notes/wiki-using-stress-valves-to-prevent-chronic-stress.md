## Brain science in tackling stress

When discussing my [simple formula for a happy life](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Simple_formula_for_a_happy_life "Simple formula for a happy life") with skeptics, I noticed that the main objection they had was the impact of emotion on the ability to stick with the formula. The emotions such as anxiety, stress, or anger were mentioned as possible roadblocks. The toughest nut to crack seems to be depression that disables all brain's defenses and turns a happy life into a potential nightmare.

It is generally assumed that inborn personality determines our ability to tackle emotions. I believe that a more important influence is environmental. The impact of childhood and upbringing can make or break a human mind. Maternal separation, child abuse, trauma, stress at school, bullying, failed early romantic relationship, etc. have the ability to potentiate one's emotional responsiveness to the environment. My childhood was free and pretty happy. I always found it easy to tackle emotions. It may be then unfair to claim that my formula for happiness is pretty tight. However, I noticed that few people seem to understand the power of the [circadian cycle](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian_cycle "Circadian cycle"), and the use of pain. Those tools are handy when addressing negative emotions too.

## Anti-stress formula

I observe that it is possible to (1) expose oneself to a stressful event at the right time, (2) contain the stress, and (3) redirect the resulting energy into resolving a problem. [Acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") may increase, but this may turn out to be a good thing. It should be possible to crank up the pain of exercise to cover up for preceding stress. A well-timed [siesta](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Siesta "Siesta") should then be able to reset the brain to the baseline as long as the pain of exercise was sufficiently strong in comparison to the level of acute stress. In other words, exercise and sleep are two different worlds of escape. These are two initial [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") that block "mental energy" on the way from the negative emotions towards a further disruption. If there are any surviving remnants of negative emotion by the evening, there is still an escape into efficient learning or high productivity. They also tend to take the mind away from the problems of the day and channel the mental energy into something productive. Last but not least, night time sleep is the most efficient [stress valve](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") that should put a stop to the toughest painful emotions in a healthy brain, in a well-managed life, most of the time. Naturally, we all know that stress is also a powerful force that can undermine that last valve. Stress is the main destroyer of healthy sleep. The battle with a high degree of stress is never easy.

Several [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") help prevent converting [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") into [chronic stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Chronic_stress "Chronic stress"). These are: (1) combative quest for solutions, (2) tough exercise, (3) [siesta](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Siesta "Siesta"), (4) productivity, and (5) **night sleep**

## Optimizing the timing of stress

In [optimizing the timing of brainwork](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Optimizing_the_timing_of_brainwork "Optimizing the timing of brainwork"), I insist that there is an optimum time of the day in which the [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") should be handled. The optimum timing is determined by two time constraints:

- [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") should be tackled early to leave room for the deployment of a maximum number of [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valves "Stress valves")
- morning creative slot should be free from acute stress (if possible)

The need to avoid stress in the early morning comes from the following reasons:

- productive morning primes the brain for efficient operation in conditions of stress
- paradoxically, **creative brain may compound stress** in problem solving, and a degree of [homeostatic](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeostatic "Homeostatic") fatigue may be beneficial
- if stress entails the sense of wasted time, a productive morning is a form of a "proactive [stress valve](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") "

If [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") slot displaces the exercise slot, it can be much harder to deploy the remaining evening [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valves "Stress valves").

The need to avoid stress before and during the evening creative slot (if possible), comes from the following reasons:

- stress that precedes [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity") without a [stress valve](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") will always have a negative impact on the productive output (no supreme master of stoic self-control can bring the cost to zero)
- undisturbed evening creative slot is a powerful [stress valve](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve")
- remnants of evening stress may affect the sleep in the night, which may be the first step towards [chronic stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Chronic_stress "Chronic stress")

The need to avoid stress before sleep is probably obvious to everyone. This should also complete the reasoning on the optimization of the [circadian time](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian_time "Circadian time") for stress.

The best time to handle [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") is the time before exercise, midday meal, and [siesta](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Siesta "Siesta")

To effectively manage the exposure to stressors at the right time, it is necessary to employ some tools of isolation from the outside world in protected time slots (e.g. turning off the cell phone, turning off e-mail, etc.). I use [Plan](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Plan "Plan") in [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo") to optimize the timing of stressors and creativity (see: [Planning a perfect productive day without stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Planning_a_perfect_productive_day_without_stress "Planning a perfect productive day without stress")).

The [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") that block negative emotions may fail on occasion. They will not stop the pain of the death of a close relative. In those cases, natural brain defenses to [chronic stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Chronic_stress "Chronic stress") will kick in and result in a degree of brain cell death. However, the pain will pass eventually. Natural [chronic stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Chronic_stress "Chronic stress") defenses are strongest in those who can escape chronic stress on most days. A healthy brain is helpful in combatting stress.

Healthy brain and [stress resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_resilience "Stress resilience") live in a positive feedback loop: one facilitates the other

The opposite of the positive feedback loop of [stress resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_resilience "Stress resilience"), is the analogous feedback loop of stress vulnerability. [Chronic stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Chronic_stress "Chronic stress") may ravage all brain structures involved in learning (e.g. as evidenced by a measurable decline in the size of the hippocampus). This undermines one of the chief defenses against stressors: the ability to learn. For this reason, minor changes in lifestyle may result in major changes in the balance of forces. In theory, the same individual, in the long run, may be tipped into rampant creativity or into clinical depression. This is why circadian management of stress, [natural creativity cycle](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Natural_creativity_cycle "Natural creativity cycle"), and the concept of simple living may be of monumental importance for the brain health.

Minor changes in lifestyle can result in major changes in brain health outcomes

## The power of stoicism

Stoicism and meditation are well-known weapons against stress. However, these are not always the best weapons when dealing with solvable problems. It is great to be stoic about the woes of the world we have no impact on. However, a personal problem may call for a different strategy. Instead of silencing [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") by weakening their association with stress response centers, we can do the opposite. We can brainstorm into a solution in the state of [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress"). This will increase activations, increase chances for a solution, and increase the chances for a positive bonus that comes with a resolution even if the resolution is based on failure (in that latter case, stoicism comes handy again). Instead of silencing the negative energy with dampened valuations, we can channel the same energy into a brain action. If the right circadian approach is taken, by the end of the day, the brain may be in a better or happier state than on a day without problems. That's the paradox of a rebound in the formula for happiness. Pain is often the best source of pleasure to follow.

If the same problem hits at the wrong [circadian time](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian_time "Circadian time"), e.g. before a creative slot, stoicism may be the only recourse. However, even the most stoic brain of a supreme guru of self-control will not avoid a negative influence of stress on efficient learning or creativity. A remedy to the disruption will always be on the spectrum from active distraction, to passive non-specific dampening of [concept map](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") activations, which undermines creativity (for more on [concept map activations](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map_activation "Concept map activation") see: [How to solve any problem?](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/How_to_solve_any_problem%3F "How to solve any problem)).

Instead of employing stoicism, proper [circadian timing](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian_phase "Circadian phase") makes it possible to channel the energy of [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") into a resolution

## Limiting stress

It should be pretty obvious that the first step towards a life with less stress and more creativity is to optimize one's trajectory towards the conditions of limited stress. One of the prime objectives is **simple living**. Everyone should try the beauty of a monkish life in a wooden cabin away from the noise of the city. A single week in spartan conditions can open one's eyes to the stifling nature of the modern world. A great deal of people suffer noisy lives filled up with stress and bad sleep only because they never knew any better. All it takes is to swap one vacation in a noisy resort into a week of isolation (see: [creative vacation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creative_vacation "Creative vacation")). Human nature comes to front and instantly shows the sources of stress in life. It also reveals the power of simple happy things like the smell of the woods, or a conversation with an old wise villager. **Simple living** does not need to mean "primitive living". It only implies a reduced complexity of the web of life. It can range from fewer things cluttering one's desk, to fewer (and deeper) social connections, to fewer jobs on a tasklist, fewer e-mails lingering in the Inbox, etc. I like to delegate complexities to [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo"). For example, I use [Plan](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Plan "Plan") to compress noisy jobs and acute stress (to leave more room for creative pursuits). I use [tasklists](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Tasklist "Tasklist") to dump in thousands of jobs. This makes it possible to focus on the one that is most important at the moment (see: [rational procrastination](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Rational_procrastination "Rational procrastination")). I use [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading") to retain sanity in the richness of knowledge available on the web. I also use [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading") to handle mail overload, and creative overload. Too many ideas can lead to a creative chaos. It may help to engage in [incremental writing](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_writing "Incremental writing") to coalesce creativity into coherent models. **Simple living** makes it easier to be stoic about life.

**Simple living** reduces the onslaught of stress, and makes it easy to handle it with [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valves "Stress valves")

## Acute stress vs. simple living

There is no contradiction between the ideas of simple living, [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") buildup, and the use of pain. They all contribute to less stress. The call for compressing all acute stress into a single stress slot comes from the fact that no life is perfect, and there are always tradeoffs between simplicity and stress. I like to use my computer to simplify my life, but when the computer fails, it is bound to entail stress.

If we accept the inevitability of stress, adding it all up into a single punch with a great deal of pain is the right way to go.

Few people see Donald Trump as their role model, however, his brain is of the kind that thrives in conditions of stress and conflict. Trump must have mastered the secret of converting negative energy into action (see: [Mystery of Donald Trump's brain](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Mystery_of_Donald_Trump%27s_brain "Mystery of Donald Trump's brain")). Not all negativity needs to end up in a heart attack. It should be possible to redirect the negative energy and give it the right outlet. This can be best done with the use of the right [circadian timing](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian_time "Circadian time") and the [safety valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") that stave off the real culprit: [chronic stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Chronic_stress "Chronic stress"), which is the actual killer.

## How do stress valves work?

The primary mechanism by which [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") work on a given day is to deprive stress of its resources. They also work in the long-run by diverting [concept map activations](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map_activation "Concept map activation") towards productive pursuits.

The stress begins with the activation of [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") that are associated with threats to one's well-being. The evaluation of threats is similar to that of the valuation of knowledge (see: [knowledge valuation network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation_network "Knowledge valuation network")). In networked activations, some of the [maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") are associated with the brain centers responsible for the response to a threat (e.g. the amygdala). The first reaction to a threat is a stress response, which may be perceived as mildly unpleasant (for minor threats) up the scale to horrifying. In natural conditions, the threat is often tackled promptly and the body can return to its usual programs such as feeding, reproduction, or creative pursuits. In modern world, we are bombarded with minor threats that can build up into major anxiety that, in the long-run, can lead to a pathology that may turn into depression.

If we cannot tackle the threat, we need to tackle the stress response. For that purpose we can:

- **suppress** the [activation of concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Activation_of_concept_maps "Activation of concept maps") that trigger the stress response (e.g. with stoic philosophy, meditation, etc.)
- **starve** the stress response from its hormonal or energetic resources (e.g. with exercise)
- **redirect** [concept map activations](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map_activation "Concept map activation") (e.g. with learning, problem solving, etc.)
- **contain** the stress-related [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") by building up a network of new connections around the concept map that will provide plausible pathways towards a solution

All [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") use one or more of the above approaches. In addition, it is important to string the stress valves in the right sequence in compliance with the [circadian cycle](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian_cycle "Circadian cycle").

In my own [stress resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_resilience "Stress resilience") strategy, I use all available methods in pre-determined sequence hooked up to the [natural creativity cycle](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Natural_creativity_cycle "Natural creativity cycle"). There are other possible approaches with a different timing, however, I believe the optimum strategy is as follows:

- **invite stress**: expose oneself to the sources of stress in optimum [circadian frame](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian_frame "Circadian frame"), i.e. around the midday on a standard western schedule
- **resolve**: attempt to resolve the problem; possible failure will provide new knowledge, and the associated [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") will deplete some of the stress response resources
- **starve**: exercise should provide further depletion of resources. If it is driven to exhaustion, and combined with the [homeostatic fatigue](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeostatic "Homeostatic"), it should non-specifically deactivate all [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") (including those associated with stress). The duration and the intensity of the exercise, as well as the level of inflicted pain, should be proportional to the level of stress
- **contain**: [siesta](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Siesta "Siesta") may be hard to execute on a stressful day. This is why utter depletion and exhaustion can be extremely helpful. Arrival of deep sleep is the brain's first chance to build new positive connections that will allow for the first revaluation of prospects for a solution
- **redirect** and **contain**: [homeostatically](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeostatic "Homeostatic") refreshed brain should be amenable to learning, creativity, and problem solving. This takes the mind away from problems (redirection), and makes it possible to build new knowledge useful in problem resolution (containment)
- **contain**: night time sleep, if not disrupted by stress, provides the best way to contain all negative activations. [Memory optimization in sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Memory_optimization_in_sleep "Memory optimization in sleep") has creative resolution of problems as one of its main objectives. Bad dreams are born from those negative activations collected during the day. Luckily, in healthy sleep, we do not experience dreams. When we wake up, those dreams bring fruit in the shape of new creative solutions available upon awakening. Death of a relative cannot be reversed, however, new ways of celebrating his or her life come up in the morning (or a few mornings after). If all [stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") worked as planned, the brain will be ready for a new bright day.

[Stress valves](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_valve "Stress valve") prevent [chronic stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Chronic_stress "Chronic stress") by building up connections that lead to a plausible resolution or to a re-interpretation of problems
