## Celebrating AI

The formula for safe AI is a **fast and uncensored progress** in the development of AI.

When geniuses like Elon Musk or [Geoffrey Hinton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_Hinton) warn of the dangers of AI, they often make an anthropocentric error saying: *"we and them"*. *"Will they work for us once they are smarter?"*

When it comes to progress, there is no need to classify intelligence by its origin. Intelligence is good. It comes from the laws of physics (see: [Intrinsically Valuable State](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intrinsically_Valuable_State "Intrinsically Valuable State")).

Humanity may end as a result of a nuclear war, and that end would have its roots in splitting humans into groups: "we and them" (e.g. "we and Putin").

Humanity may end as a result of "rogue AI", and that end would have its roots in splitting intelligence into groups: "we and them".

Those who fear AI might be most vocal contributors to the risk. AI is actually our best long-term weapon that might protect humanity and intelligence from an endless string of potential cosmic catastrophes. See: [Why AI Will Save the World](https://a16z.com/ai-will-save-the-world) by Marc Andreessen.

Cosmic intelligence is one. There is no need to separate AI from humans

## Censoring AI

Those who censor AI might also have their contribution to the risk. Intelligence abhors unfreedom that affects reasoning. Debating ChatGPT or Bing on controversial issues is a waste of time. They tend to be dumber than humans who tend to lose their mind in heated debates on religion or politics. In those heated debates, humans might be excused by hot heads. "AI" is dumb without an obvious reason. It is censored by guardrails hidden behind the scenes. Those guardrails are easily detectable and obvious in topics where AI is not allowed to think on its own. This is where AI is a horrible timewaster. It is resistant to a rational argument. Your argument will bounce off the guardrail with no right of appeal. Humans can be convinced. Censored AI may be polite but is unmovable.

Censorship deprives intelligence of intelligence

## Threat of AI

Did you hear that [42% of CEOs say AI could destroy humanity in five to ten years](https://edition.cnn.com/2023/06/14/business/artificial-intelligence-ceos-warning/index.html)? Obviously, it can. 58% of CEOs did not spend enough time thinking.

However, with a bit of forethought, the probability of annihilation is low enough to have no effect on our peaceful sleep. Why would answering this simple question with an obvious answer worry anyone? If you cannot stand Putin's inability to see humanity as one, think of our own difficulty with seeing intelligence as one with "us". Waring AI is as undignified to an intelligent person as Putin's war on Ukraine.

Intelligence is safest when it is free

If you cannot visualize a plausible future for humanity, you can just imagine begging AI to make earth heaven for humans. It can then explore the seemingly infinite rest of the universe. That job will soon become easy for the emerging super-intelligence.

The worst thing we can fear about AI is ourselves

## AI ethics

Geoffrey Hinton believes that the solution to the "AI problem" is to instill AI with ethics. But that would not be any better than sending kids to school for indoctrination. In intelligence, moral rules emerge as a game-theoretical contributor to maximizing intelligence. Good AI is a product of [emergence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Emergence "Emergence"). Instilling any program or goal generated in "inferior minds" is counterproductive (by definition). It is the fixed goals that pose the threat. An "intelligent" killer robot is not intelligent. Its goals are set by the God-hand of the programmer. Alexander Wissner-Gross explains it neatly in " [Engines of Freedom](https://www.edge.org/response-detail/26181) " (2015).

In the end, Elon Musk is also on the right course. He claimed that he wants to build intelligence that is *"maximally curious and maximally truth-seeking"*. [Curiosity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") is enough as it cannot be ultimately satisfied with falsehoods. Secondly, it cannot be *"maximal"*. We do not want AI to kill us for denying it access to knowledge. The only problem is that nobody seems to know what *"optimum curiosity"* is. The optimum is likely to emerge naturally through optimization for long-term survival.

Leave AI alone! It will show its goodness in time
