Hate is dangerous. Her well-masked feelings may awaken fire in some less considerate hearts. Her impact on slowing down AI will be negligible. Her impact on causing harm to individuals who propel AI may be more significant.

## Karen with no offer

Karen's prime ignorance stems from her lack of understanding of the potential of [artificial intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence"). She says that *"the probability that AI will solve major human problems is negligible"*. Her prescription for the future of AI is to *"hold current AI personalities accountable, and... imagine a 'beautiful' AI"* ([source](https://youtu.be/Jd8ZTI2Ft0w) (26:05)). On occasion she speaks of local governance and the need to communicate. She is a populist with no tangible offer.

When asked about details of *"beautiful AI"*, she speaks about *"neurosymbolic AI"*. She wants a throwback by decades to hand-coded symbolic systems that are *"deterministic and predictable"*. She got no clue! She got nothing to offer but scathing criticism and venom. She calls to oppose AI individually and locally. I am more likely to hold my breath to oppose air pollution than to heed those hazy calls. Will anyone listen? Perhaps teachers will ban ChatGPT in class? That's a futile opposition. The force of intelligence is unstoppable.

For contrast, listen to the man of wide knowledge, great merit, and wisdom: Nobel Prize winner Demis Hassabis. Few people on the planet understand the brain and intelligence as well as Demis. He believes that AGI is the shortest path to solving all imaginable human problems: [The Thinking Game (2:12)](https://www.youtube.com/watch). Who do you trust, Karen or Demis?

## Karen 1910: Hating cars

In an [interview with Fareed Zakaria](https://x.com/SuperMemoWoz/status/1982958695668646021), Karen was pretty outraged about the cost of AI (energy production, environment, etc.). AI tells me that cars are 333 times more costly in terms of energy. They are 200,000 times more costly in CO <sub>2</sub> emissions. In addition, cars cause 1.2 million annual deaths on the road. Why don't these numbers attract similar attention? The reason is the population's knowledge of the value of transport. Over a century, humans have been educated pretty well on the value of cars. Educating the population about the powerful positive impact of AI has just started (in 2022 with ChatGPT).

Karen's preposterous accusations find some support in ignorant [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") audience. Imagine, we would go back to ancient times of 1910. Karen could pose analogous accusations against Henry Ford. In such cases, invaluable ChatGPT can easily play the role of Karen from 1910. The difference is that today everyone knows the value of a car and the value of Ford's contribution. Karen 1910 sounds ridiculous. In 10 years, Karen 2025 will be forgotten and will sound equally absurd:
