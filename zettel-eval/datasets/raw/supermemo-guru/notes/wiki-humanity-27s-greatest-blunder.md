## Greatest blunder

What idea intended to do good did most harm to humanity? There are many contenders. I asked this question on one of educational Facebook groups and got some inspiring feedback. A typical bad idea might be Thalidomide: a drug intended to help women with morning sickness that turned out to cause horrible birth defects in up to 20,000 children. It took some five years to uncover the blunder. However, Thalidomide did not get many votes. Perhaps it was largely forgotten in the 60 years that have passed. There was not much support for deforestation or the dropping of the atom bomb. Below I list a few contenders that were accused of doing most harm.

## Contenders

### War

War is a game-theoretical side effect of conflict. If it is a blunder, it is a blunder of individuals or groups lead by individuals. Once started, wars are hard to stop. They cannot be considered a true contender as there was never a global consensus that wars are a good idea. They are usually seen as the last resort.

### Environmental destruction

Enviromental destruction is a side effect of progress. Depletion of the Aral Sea might be a result of ignorance. Release of CO2 is often a side effect of what is inevitable or necessary. Enviromental destruction is widely condemned, but it is also hard to prevent as we prioritize progress. Progress is a natural consequence of the [force of intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Force_of_intelligence "Force of intelligence"). Destroying the environment is not a blunder as nobody ever claimed it was a good idea.

### Religion

Some people claim religion is the greatest blunder. Religion was the winner of my poll. Crimes in the name of God are independent blunders on their own. Religion has provided solace and guidance to millions for millennia. It is a mixed bag of good and bad influences. Today it is becoming a harmless and voluntary factor of human spiritual life. The demand is diminishing, and the role of religions is fading in modern societies.

### Slavery

There have always been strong moral objections to slavery. It has never been sealed as a good idea with no ethical qualms. It survived for centuries as it brought benefits to individuals, groups of people or states. Slavery is rather not a blunder. It is a reason for shame. We have always known it is a morally corrupt idea. Naturally, not all people are equipped with a moral compass.

### Communism

Communism might have cost 100 million human lives. Unlike Holocaust, it was not a criminal enterprise invented by evil individuals. The ideology of egalitarian community is noble. The idea of sharing is universal. Communism was a response to heartless side effects of capitalism. After a bit of indoctrination at school in communist Poland, I was a good-hearted follower as well. For the idea to do damage, it had to mutate into a totalitarian ideology. It is the totalitarianism that did the damage of Stalin, Mao or Pol Pot. Perhaps **totalitarianism** would be a better contender?

What makes matters worse, [society as a concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Society_as_a_concept_network "Society as a concept network") governed by [neurocracy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neurocracy "Neurocracy") might resurrect a great deal of communist ideas. Marx's phrase *"From each according to his ability, to each according to his needs"* is implemented in the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") of the brain. Neurons do not care about wealth. They do their best and consume only as much as they truly need.

Before we arrive at that stage, [artificial intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence") might make it harder for youth to enter the job market. That will make [basic income](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Basic_income "Basic income") a necessary transition tool.

It is not the ideals of communism that did the damage. Their impact on hearts and minds cannot be seen as a blunder. Totalitarian states emerged individually, and the blame should be with the bloody dictators who sent millions to gulags and committed other totalitarian crimes.

Totalitarianism was responsible for the toll of communism

## School coercion

True blunders are the effect of bad ideas that affect many. In my poll, nobody mentioned [coercive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_schooling "Coercive schooling").

Here I will argue that [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling") is humanity's worst blunder. Its intent is pure and grand. Its costs are horrible. However, what makes it a winner is the fact that (1) few people truly understand the extent of the damage, (2) the damage is horrendous, and (3) the damage keeps increasing! Most people still see schools not as a blunder, but as a noble way to enlightenment! Unlike in communism, the idea does not need to mutate, and be corrupted. Coercively putting kids in a classroom was the original intent and part of the plan.

The [coercive school system](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling") is humanity's greatest blunder. Its implications are wide-raging and cost trillions. Communism never passed 20% global support at its heyday, and in its criminal mutations it lasted "only" 70 years.

Compulsory schooling is today the most powerful enemy of progress

### Mechanism

When a rat is closed in a cage, he quickly progresses towards [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"). When humans are closed in the cage of a classroom, the progression is less obvious. However, over years, it indeed contributes to [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"). If you ask an average kid, why he goes to school even if he hates it, he will just say *"I have to"*. He treats coercion like a concrete wall that will be there forever. That's a classic set up for [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"), which in turn leads to [depression](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Depression "Depression"), [addictions](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Addiction "Addiction"), [suicide](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Suicide "Suicide"), etc.

In addition to destroying mental health, coercive school leads to over a [100 bad habits](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/100_bad_habits "100 bad habits") that make life difficult or miserable. The problem is that [school mythology](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neuromythology "Neuromythology") perfected over the last 200 years, shifts the blame from the limits on freedom to affected students themselves, to social media, to families, to drug cartels, etc.

Limits on freedom have [disastrous effects on mental health](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Gray:_Limits_on_freedom_lead_to_depression_and_suicide "Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide")

### Drug wars

War on drugs costs $100 billion annually. It is a classic side effect of the education blunder. School destroys [resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resilience "Resilience") and contributes to [reward deprivation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Reward_deprivation "Reward deprivation"). This is a perfect breeding ground for addictions. It is not that drugs are evil. It is that a destroyed human mind cannot resist the lure. War on drugs is a guaranteed failure. The profits are just too cosmic. Humans adapt to market opportunities. The only way to stop the epidemic of addictions is to resurrect social mental health. The first step is to stop oppressive childhood, incl. [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling").

The best way to win the war on drugs is to **terminate oppressive childhood**

### School shootings

School shootings grew to be a nearly daily occurrence. Only more dramatic cases make the news, and they usually end with increasingly meaningless *"Our thoughts and prayers are with you"*. A knee reflex response is to limit access to guns, which would obviously limit casualties. However, the only working remedy is to end school coercion. Kids who hate school should never be imprisoned there. School is a place of pathological [closed system socialization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Closed_system_socialization "Closed system socialization"). Yesterday's shooter in [Trump](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Trump "Trump") assassination attempt was [bullied at school](https://www.youtube.com/watch) (July 13, 2024). See: [Bullying](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Bullying "Bullying")

The root cause of school shootings is the coercive school itself

### Suicide

More and more kids decide to end their lives. Adults blame other adults, other kids, or [social media](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Social_media "Social media"). The supposed solution is better psychiatric care (e.g. more psychologists at schools). In reality, the straight path from [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") via depression to suicide is fueled primarily by coercion, with school coercion at the lead (see: [Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Gray:_Limits_on_freedom_lead_to_depression_and_suicide "Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide")). We might equally well close all potential suicide victims in padded cages. That would solve the suicide problem but leave the damage to mental health unchanged.

[The end to compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/The_end_to_compulsory_schooling "The end to compulsory schooling") would dramatically reduce suicide rates

### Social media

Kids fascinated with YouTube or computer games find school horribly boring. This results in family wars about [screens](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Screens "Screens"). Teachers become sworn enemies of social media (see: [Ban schools, not TikTok](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Ban_schools,_not_TikTok "Ban schools, not TikTok")). Dozens of research papers aim to prove that social media is harmful for the youth. Allegedly, social media is responsible for the epidemic of suicide (for the true explanation see: [Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Gray:_Limits_on_freedom_lead_to_depression_and_suicide "Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide")). Social media are said to stunt the brains (see: [Digital Dementia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Digital_Dementia "Digital Dementia")). In reality, social media is entirely harmless for a [resilient mind](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resilience "Resilience"), i.e. the default mind of a healthy free child or adult.

### Disabilities

The idea of school is to boost skills, knowledge and [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"). However, [school coercion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_coercion "School coercion") leads directly to [dyslexia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dyslexia "Dyslexia"), dyscalculia, hate of learning and/or [ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/ADHD "ADHD") diagnosis. For explanations see: [How school coercion generates dyslexia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schools_contribute_to_the_dyslexia_epidemic "Schools contribute to the dyslexia epidemic") and [How creative kids are branded ADHD](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Confusing_creativity_with_ADHD "Confusing creativity with ADHD") (or [Asperger](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Asperger "Asperger")).

Schools fuel a ginormous industry of learning disabilities

### Eating disorders

Eating disorders are the direct effect of the decline in the mental health of youth. Obesity is natural in kids woken up prematurely. [Sleep deficits](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep_deficit "Sleep deficit") discourage exercise. Long school hours foster immobility. PE is involuntary, mis-timed and often mis-matched (volleyball is no fun for a soccer afficionado). Instead of promoting exercise, PE classes often result in conditioning a dislike for exercise. Schools are the [main cause of obesity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Schools_are_the_primary_source_of_obesity_epidemic "Schools are the primary source of obesity epidemic"). In conjunction with a declining mental health, obesity or low self-esteem may lead to anorexia or bulimia.

Freedom is essential for healthy eating and exercise

### Conspiracy theories

Conspiracy theories result from a backlash against a [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") mainstream reasoning. After many years of oppressive indoctrination, the opposite points of view taste great.

In addition, due to the reliance on the only truth dished out by the authority, one of the side effects of schooling is a decline in critical thinking. School hurts [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence")

Conspiracy theories thrive on [reactance](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Reactance "Reactance"). Not on ignorance!
