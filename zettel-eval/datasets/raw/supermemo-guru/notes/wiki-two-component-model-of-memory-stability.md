## Two-component model of memory

Long-term memory is characterized by two essential variables: [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") and [retrievability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Retrievability "Retrievability") (see: [two-component model of long-term memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Two-component_model_of_long-term_memory "Two-component model of long-term memory")). New data from molecular neuroscience hints at the possibility that memory stability itself has two components: [molecular and structural](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Two_components_of_memory_stability "Two components of memory stability"). The [two-component model of memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Two-component_model_of_memory "Two-component model of memory") is based on statistical data obtained in the process of learning based on [spaced repetition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition"). The new hypothesis on the two components of stability is largely theoretical and hard to confirm with presently available statistical data. However, [neurohistological evidence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Dendritic_spine_stabilization_corresponds_with_long-term_memories "Dendritic spine stabilization corresponds with long-term memories") keeps mounting.

## Two-component model of stability

Historically, we have always hypothesized that [memory stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Memory_stability "Memory stability") would be based on a buildup of proteins in the [PSD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postsynaptic_density). Over decades, [AMPA receptors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AMPA_receptor) became the strongest candidates for that role and the evidence keeps adding up. In parallel, research has shown more clearly that new synaptic connections may form in the wake of learning. New connections seem too costly to play a role in propping up [memory stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Memory_stability "Memory stability") at each [review](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Review "Review"). Protein buildup is simply cheaper in metabolic terms and easier to optimize with evolutionary forces. New connections, on the other hand, could play role in effectual connectivity. New [concept maps](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_map "Concept map") established in memory should have the ability to establish more effective connections that could serve information processing. In other words, new synapses would contribute to **connectivity**, not to **[stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability")**. However, a side effect of new connectivity might be an increase in memory [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence"), which in turn is known to have a dramatic impact on the survival of memories. [Stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") might be propped up in [processes that occur in sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neural_optimization_in_sleep "Neural optimization in sleep"). **[Neural optimization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neural_optimization_in_sleep "Neural optimization in sleep")** in sleep seems to depend highly on memory [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence").

This lead to a hypothesis that [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") may have two components: (1) **molecular** and (2) **structural**. At this point, we have no way to measure those components outwardly other than in full convolution. Properties of memory in [spaced repetition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition") make it only possible to see stability as a single variable. However, it is worth noting that [stability tends to increase dramatically](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stabilization_curve "Stabilization curve") with review early in the process, and approach asymptotically very low levels of [stability increase](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability_increase "Stability increase") decades later. This could be explained by the fact that a memory finds its place in the overall knowledge structure of the individual, achieves high [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability"), and is then minimally responsive to the effects of further retrieval. In other words, new memories might stimulate sprouting of new synaptic connections, while continuing the build up of receptors responsible for molecular stability (see: [AMPA receptors stabilize the dendritic branch](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/AMPA_receptors_stabilize_the_dendritic_branch "AMPA receptors stabilize the dendritic branch")).

New ideas may enhance structural stability. Review may enhance molecular stability

## Conclusions

- in [two component model of long-term memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Two_component_model_of_long-term_memory "Two component model of long-term memory"), [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") might itself be a two process phenomenon: molecular and structural
- optimum timing of review will have a [decreasing impact on memory stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stabilization_curve "Stabilization curve") over the lifetime of a memory
- high [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") memories may be best established with the help of: [optimum review](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition"), high [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence"), and [sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep "Sleep")
- [sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep "Sleep") may be most effective in reinforcing memories when there is a combined impact of both [optimum review](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition") and increased [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence")
