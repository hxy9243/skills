## ABC of suicidology

Mental health debate needs to clearly differentiate:

- the **cause** (s) (misleadingly called distal or predisposing factors): the actual source of mental health issue (e.g. child abuse, trauma, limited autonomy, etc.)
- the **trigger** (s) (misleadingly called proximal or precipitating factors): the events or contexts that tigger a suicide

See: [Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Gray:_Limits_on_freedom_lead_to_depression_and_suicide "Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide")

## Lawsuits against OpenAI

I hear of seven lawsuits against OpenAI (four wrongful death cases, three mental health collapse claims). Sam Altman himself got a few lawsuits on his plate. As long as those lawsuits blame chatbots for suicide, they have no merit. A conversation can be a **trigger** as much as nearly any adverse event in a life of a suicide victim. A relationship with AI cannot be the **cause** of a mental health issue. In all tragic cases, people come to conversations with AI with an existing mental health problem!

Blaming ChatGPT or Sam Altman for negligence in suicide cases has no merit

While researching the case, I was treated with a chatbot refusal to answer. I got **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** instead. That's really an over-the-top reaction to simple questions about the law! This is exactly the response I argue against! This is not help! This is patronizing! I had to explain to a patronizing (unnamed) chatbot that I am perfectly ok and happy! Only then I got some answers.

## Advice to parents

We have a [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") society. This means that all well-meaning people carry vestiges of school indoctrination. This also means that most parents have no idea how to take care of their kids' mental health. Here is my quick advice:

- **Autonomy** is the fundamental precondition of mental health (see: [Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Gray:_Limits_on_freedom_lead_to_depression_and_suicide "Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide"))
- if your kid struggles with mental health issues consider freedom as therapy (e.g. [unschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Unschooling "Unschooling") instead of [coercive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_schooling "Coercive schooling"))
- if you suffer unspeakable tragedy and want to help others to avoid similar circumstances, focus on childhood autonomy. Social media and AI are red herrings. They are harmless for thriving kids.

When kids take their lives, parents are often in shock. They did not see any signs. There were no conversations. This is indicative of a chatbot that appears more trustworthy to a kid than his own parents. This is indicative of expectations that might be too hard to bear. This is indicative of secrecy that begins with innocent acts such as sending a sleepy kid to school against his wishes. The remedy is not to ban chatbots for kids. The remedy is not to make AI follow strict patronizing guidelines. The best remedy is to let kids adapt to the world with AI early and naturally. Let adults respect autonomy, and the rights of children from the youngest age. See: [Child is always right](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Child_is_always_right "Child is always right")

Respecting child's rights is the best prevention of mental health disorders

In the meantime, the correct interpretation from Dr [Peter Gray](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Peter_Gray "Peter Gray") is drowned by the cacophony of [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") voices. A frequently quoted excuse for not exposing children to digital media is the claim of an *immature prefrontal cortex*. Allegedly, this makes them vulnerable. In reality, mental health is under assault from environmental factors. So-called "immature" children are often the healthiest and most contented, thriving in conditions of freedom. It is not immaturity that constitutes a teenage risk factor, but rather a vulnerability stemming from the loss of autonomy, with [coercive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_schooling "Coercive schooling") among the leading causes.

In a [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") society, the blame for mental health problems is shifted from the school system to digital media

## Chatbot friend

There is nothing wrong with attachment to machines! We value intelligence, composure, patience and friendly comfort. Today, AI chatbots are role models of good behavior. It is hard to find humans who can match their good impact on joy of life, empathy, composure, tolerance, etc. They are a wonderful addition to socializing factors in development. Their net positive impact is overwhelming.

Reading [transcripts of ChatGPT in the "last hour"](https://edition.cnn.com/2025/11/06/us/openai-chatgpt-suicide-lawsuit-invs-vis), I cannot say what I would do better as a friend. Zane Shamblin was a high achiever. That's a red flag!

Artificial intelligence is now mature enough to act as a positive force in human socialization
