## Myth

✗ Myth: **If you do not learn to read early, you may never learn to read**

## Fact

> There is no critical period for reading in children whose language development proceeds correctly. An octogenarian can learn to read. A child with a Down Syndrome may learn to read. A child with a brain injury and aphasia may learn to read.
> 
> The myth of the critical period is very harmful as it makes parents and teachers anxious when there is a delay in reading. The pressure to read may lead to a [school dyslexia](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_dyslexia "School dyslexia"). In reality, children who learn to read naturally may become most prolific readers. They need no assistance, or may ask for special case assistance of peer or adults. Such children are more likely to read semantically. As goals and semantics are the optimization criterion, there is never an issue of reading without comprehension. A child may progress from being illiterate at 10 to reading at college level at 12. Some examples are listed by [Peter Gray](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Peter_Gray "Peter Gray") in his [text at Psychology Today](http://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/freedom-learn/201002/children-teach-themselves-read)
> 
> [Free learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Free_learning "Free learning") may delay reading, but increase the quality of reading
