## Definition of free learning

**Free learning** is learning in which all choices are made by the student. Free learning is usually based on [self-learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Self-learning "Self-learning") that is [self-directed](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Self-directed_learning "Self-directed learning") and [self-paced](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Self-paced_learning "Self-paced learning"). In **free learning** there are no constraints of time, space, pace, method, direction, or subject matter. **Free learning** is defined as the polar opposite of **[passive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Passive_schooling "Passive schooling")** or **[direct instruction](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Direct_instruction "Direct instruction")**.

## Efficiency of free learning

By definition, **free learning** is optimum learning. It complies with the [Fundamental law of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Fundamental_law_of_learning "Fundamental law of learning"). The optimality is derived from the [pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning").

The advantages of **free learning** over **[passive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Passive_schooling "Passive schooling")** can be illustrated with the [crystallization metaphor](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_crystallization "Knowledge crystallization"):

Crystallization metaphor of schooling and unschooling

> ***Figure:** In **perfect schooling** we create a perfect crystal of knowledge. In college, we add an extra crystal of specialization. In reality though, learning looks a bit less perfect. For most kids, knowledge never builds sufficient [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence") and falls apart due to [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference") (i.e. fast [forgetting](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Forgetting "Forgetting")). As a result, in **real schooling**, knowledge asymptotically reaches a certain volume and keeps churning around from that point on with little progress in [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") or [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence"). In contrast, in **free learning**, the acquisition of knowledge is chaotic and uneven. However, as long as it is based on the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), the volume of knowledge is very large. Individual crystals of knowledge collide, and build [consistency](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Consistency "Consistency") and [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence"). This in turn helps [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability") and further integration of knowledge. By the time of college, in terms of volume, free learners should know far more than ordinary students. Free knowledge has multiple areas of strength, and multiple areas of weakness. However, it is superior in [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence"). This is why it is more applicable in [problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/How_to_solve_any_problem%3F "How to solve any problem)*

## Origins of the term

[Ivan Illich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Illich) proposed the term *[deschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Deschooling "Deschooling")*, which means " *[abolishing compulsory education](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Abolish_compulsory_education "Abolish compulsory education")* ". [John Holt](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/John_Holt "John Holt") proposed the term *[unschooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Unschooling "Unschooling")*, which means " *taking kids away from school* ". Many educators propose the term " *life learning* ", " *learning via living* " or just " *living* ". Others adhere to the concept of " *[natural learning](http://www.funderstanding.com/educators/natural-learning-brain-principles)* ", *"experience-driven learning"*, *" [discovery learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Discovery_learning "Discovery learning") "*, *"independent learning"*, or limit the debate to the concept [self-directed learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Self-directed_learning "Self-directed learning"), which is possible in prison too. Self-directed learning may be closest to the term *free learning*, however, it often raises opposition by implying that [learning needs no teacher](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Do_we_need_teachers%3F "Do we need teachers).

In addition, *free learning* has a major advantage over the terms *self-directed learning* and *self-learning*. The latter two have different definitions, can easily be confused, and are weak mnemonically. It is very easy to remember and use the term *free learning* even then when it is not defined as precisely as in this text.

[Peter Gray](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Peter_Gray "Peter Gray") in " *Free to learn* " emphasizes the importance of freedom in education and in the life of a child. Gray himself opted to promote the term *self-directed education*, which may be too unwieldy to become a good meme. Even its proponents often shorten it to *SDE*, which is a first step to make SDE texts cryptic.

Gray's philosophy is based on **freedom**, and stems from sheer **love** for children. Consequently, I became fond of the term " *free learning* " instead of *unschooling* or *deschooling* or *SDE*. It has been noticed long ago that *deschooling* and *unschooling* have many negative connotations. They are associated with rebellion and easily [raise opposition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Glorification_of_schooling "Glorification of schooling") from those who don't know the subject, but for whom knowledge or school matter. Even unschoolers object to the need to describe their formula for life by reference to schooling.

An alternative spelling for **free learning** might be **FHREE learning** where **FHREE** stands for **[Full Human Rights-Experience Education](http://www.fhree.org/)**.

**Free learning** is a positive force that brings forth learning, knowledge, happy lives, and happy society
