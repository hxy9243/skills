## Adaptability paradox

There is a seeming contradiction between two claims: (1) the brain is a perfectly adaptable device, and (2) we lose a great deal of adaptability at school. If the brain is so great, how can school damage it so easily?

*A friend noticed:* You speak that the brain is a perfectly adaptable device. You say that it makes it possible for a child to adapt to any environment. If so, how can you claim, at the same time, that children leave school poorly regulated? How do they lose adaptability and become helpless? I think the problem is related to the complexity of the world. Graduates have to confront an increasingly complex reality

There is no contradiction between adaptability and susceptibility to helplessness. [Learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") is just a form of adaptation to chaotic control (as explained below).

## Adaptation that hurts adaptability

Adaptability is a property of the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") of the brain, while the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") is just part of that network. Adaptability may involve turning off the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), and this is exactly what happens at school. Children lose their love of learning and simply stop learning. [Optimality of the learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Optimality_of_the_learn_drive "Optimality of the learn drive") is of no value if the learn drive itself is conditioned out of the control system. If adaptations lead to neuronal death or changes in brain wiring, we may end up with [irreversible maladaptability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Irreversible_maladaptability "Irreversible maladaptability").

The brain itself is not hermetic to non-adaptive injury. It can provide perfect adaptability to the survival in a concentration camp. However, once the bad nutrition starts digging into the brain protein structures, nerve cells will start leaching nutrients, lose function, or die. The control systems will go haywire. Adaptability is the territory of the brain which itself is hosted in a higher-level control ecosystem.

We can also undermine brain's adaptability in a matter of hours. A large proportion of the population is [sleep deprived](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep_deprivation "Sleep deprivation"). [Homeostatic sleep drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeostatic "Homeostatic") is a vital part of the [sleep control system](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep_control_system "Sleep control system"). However, when we override it by staying awake for too long, we indiscriminately hurt **all** control systems in the brain (including the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")). Perfect adaptability can easily be annihilated by increasing the error rate in the control device. Not only is a sleepy brain poorly adaptable. It can actually undo prior adaptations, e.g. via [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference"), due to the loss of control in the sleep deprived state (see: [Swiss cheese model of sleep deprivation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Swiss_cheese_model "Swiss cheese model")).

## Missile metaphor

The impact of school on [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") can be illustrated with an **intelligent missile metaphor**. The metaphor explains how adaptability may lead to maladaptability:

Imagine a perfectly adaptable low flying missile that can learn to navigate terrain obstacles in search of a target. Like the human brain adapts to the environment, the missile adapts to the navigation in a difficult terrain. With each hour of flight, the missile will become more and more efficient in tackling unpredictable situations. However, if we try to control the missile manually, as it happens to brains at school, the adaptation may lead to poor control. Let's imagine the missile flying into a long dark tunnel. Very soon, the missile will master the art of staying on course. In the tunnel, there is no complex control, and no advanced navigation necessary. After many years in the tunnel, when we release the missile back to the freedom of complex terrain, it will likely crash for it will have lost its navigation capacity through years of disuse. At school we do get some knowledge that may assist the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") in the process of recovery. However, this knowledge is comparable to taking on board a load of navigation manuals. After leaving the tunnel, the missile needs to navigate. Reading manuals with comprehension may come too late. Intelligent navigation requires fluency in making choices. That fluency does not directly come from textbook knowledge, and requires hours or years of practice.

See also a similar example from the world of animal learning: [On the superiority of a rat over a schooled human](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/On_the_superiority_of_a_rat_over_a_schooled_human "On the superiority of a rat over a schooled human")

The knowledge gained at school has low [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence"), low [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability"), and low [applicability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Applicability "Applicability"). From the brain's point of view, it is burdened with low [valuation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Valuation "Valuation"). This is why this type of knowledge is subject to prompt [forgetting](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Forgetting "Forgetting") via [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference"). Most of all, knowledge of low [abstractness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Abstractness "Abstractness") and low [applicability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Applicability "Applicability") will not be too helpful in boosting [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence").

The flight through the tunnel might be compared to feeding the missile with contradictory data. The [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") says *"learn chemistry"*, while the teacher says *"learn physics"*. It is as if your wife kept telling you *"I love you", "I hate you", "I love you"*, etc. At some point the wife signal will get ignored as meaningless (the brain will condition it out). This way the brain starts ignoring messages from the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") system. It becomes driven by grades and accolades, or perhaps penalties. At worst, the helpless brain focuses on just surviving one more day at school. School with its fake incentivizations is comparable to a noisy guidance input data.

If a missile receives random data, or a signal burdened with heavy noise, its control system will quickly learn to ignore the noise. It is as if the missile flew in the tunnel with nothing interesting on input. The missile loses its intelligence. When it exists the tunnel, it flies at the mercy of wind and gravity.

Paradoxically, at school, we adapt to being controllable by losing adaptability.

Schools hurt adaptability by accelerating [conceptualization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptualization "Conceptualization") (e.g. due to the effect of stress). The adaptation goes in the direction of the brain becoming a passive receptacle for knowledge. Recovery of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") in adulthood is hard because the [conceptualization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptualization "Conceptualization") process is convergent and hard to reverse due to the average increase in [memory stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Memory_stability "Memory stability") in time (see: [irreversible maladaptability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Irreversible_maladaptability "Irreversible maladaptability")).

When graduates come back to life, they are easily lost without a guiding hand of school. They have lost key ingredients of [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"). Adaptability at school is used to develop passivity.

After two decades of guidance at school, a student may find it hard to walk out into the real world

> ***Figure:** C. elegans has a nervous system made of only 302 neurons. However, this is enough to implement an [exploratory algorithm](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Exploratory_algorithm "Exploratory algorithm") that is reminiscent of human [curiosity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curiosity "Curiosity"), [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity"), and [problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving"). When the worm finds a patch of food, it will explore it. However, on occasion it will take an unexpected dash in a random direction in search of new patches (bacteria). Similar algorithms can be found in other animals, however, human [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") is far more complex. It is based on [knowledge valuation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation") and the exploratory breaks are reserved for period of [learntropy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learntropy "Learntropy") dropping well below the expected value. Human creativity emerges from complex cognitive processes informed by knowledge, in contrast to the worm's behavior, which consists solely of stochastic directional choices. For the worm, a new patch of bacteria is a problem solved, for a human it might be a new idea for terraforming Mars. Last but not least, the metaphoric tool for inducing [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") (marked as "school") in primitive animals will rather only have the form of drive habituation. Nevertheless, the little worm may present a convincing illustration that the [intelligent missile metaphor](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligent_missile_metaphor "Intelligent missile metaphor") is far more universal and may be relevant to primitive nervous systems as well.* For more on the universality of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") see: [The psychology and neuroscience of curiosity](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4635443)

## Learn drive is adaptable

We are born with a healthy [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"). A baby is curious of the world and begins to learn even before it is born.

The learn drive is powered by [knowledge valuations](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation") that are hierarchical and can be expanded with learning. The more we learn the more we see new interesting things to learn. Learning with pleasure encourages more learning. The more we learn, the more curious we get.

At first, a child may want to be an important person for egotistical reasons. He may strive to be a scientist. Soon he might find out that chemistry or physics are fun branches of science. The kid may value chemistry because it would lead to the goal. Soon he might love all kinds of explosive chemical experiments. For this Mythbuster child, the play with strong acids would sure be of prime value.

However, at school, it appears the kid is too young for chemistry, and there are lots of grammar and spelling drills to be had. There is some geography too. Grades and penalties will determine new [valuations of knowledge](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation"). The kid may want to read a chemistry book but needs to move on to the multiplication table. Due to a neural decision conflict, if the pressures of schooling are strong enough, the value of chemistry will be suppressed. Suppressed learning will have a form of networked negative valuations that [spread](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spreading_activation "Spreading activation") from the [concept](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept") of the chemistry book, via the [concepts](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept "Concept") associated with experiments, via the love of chemistry in general up in the direction of the goal of becoming a scientists. The weakest bottom links will be erased first to prevent control conflict.

Passions may be hard to extinguish, but school may be equally tough in executing its demands. In the process of reversal learning, we unlearn previous valuations. They become reversed. We may gradually lose all passions, interest in learning, interest in problem solving and re-focus on grades and reward, or on penalties, or just on surviving the school day with minimum cost in pain.

When we condition out the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), we turn off one of the main sources of reward in life. As a result, helpless adults will be vulnerable to [depression](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Depression "Depression"), and [addictions](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Addiction "Addiction").

By inducing [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"), schooling increases the risk of depression and addictions

## Knowledge devaluation

The [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") relies on [knowledge valuation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation"), which is hierarchical: the goals determine the value of sub-goals, and knowledge is valued on the basis of goals it can help achieve.

School conditioning has a form of reversal learning in which there is a conflict between the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") guidance and the systems of incentives setup by schools (for symmetry, I call it the [school drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_drive "School drive")). Researchers observe similar phenomena in learned helplessness experiments (e.g. by imprisoning rats in water tanks with no route of escape).

Ray Kurzweil described [conceptual computation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptual_computation "Conceptual computation") with the following words:

> Signals go up and down the conceptual hierarchy. A signal going up means, *“I’ve detected a pattern.”* A signal going down means, *“I’m expecting your pattern to occur,”* and is essentially a prediction. Both upward and downward signals can be either excitatory or inhibitory

Using Kurzweil's language, we might say that the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") sends an excitatory signal: *"I have detected a pattern of valuable knowledge"*. However, this signal is overridden by an inhibitory [school drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_drive "School drive") signal: *"I am expecting no value in that pattern. That knowledge is of little concern. Do not bother!"*.

The picture shows a conceptual conflict that may occur at any level of the [knowledge valuation network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation_network "Knowledge valuation network"). When school penalties are strong enough, the valuation tree will be disrupted with negative valuations propagating up towards the goals:

![](https://supermemo.guru/images/thumb/2/27/Neural_competition_between_the_learn_drive_and_the_system_of_rewards_at_school.png/500px-Neural_competition_between_the_learn_drive_and_the_system_of_rewards_at_school.png)

> ***Figure:** This is how school destroys the love of learning. **[Learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")** is the set of passions and interests that a child would like to pursue. **[School drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_drive "School drive")** is the set of rewards and penalties set up by the school system. **Learn drive** leads to simple, mnemonic, [coherent](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherent "Coherent"), [stable](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stable "Stable") and [applicable](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Applicable "Applicable") memories due to the fact that the quality of knowledge determines the degree of reward in the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") system. **School drive** leads to complex, short-term memories vulnerable to [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference") due to the fact that schools serialize knowledge by [curriculum](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curriculum "Curriculum") (not by the neural mechanism of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")). [Competitive inhibition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/War_of_the_networks "War of the networks") between the **Learn drive** and the **School drive** circuits will lead to the weakening of neural connections. Strong **School drive** will weaken the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), destroy the passion for learning, and lead to [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"). Powerful **Learn drive** will lead to [rebellion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") that will protect intrinsic passions, but possibly will also lead to problems at school. Storing new knowledge under the influence of **Learn drive** is highly rewarding and carries no penalty (by definition of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")). This will make the learn drive thrive leading to success in learning (and at school). In contrast, poor quality of knowledge induced by the pressures of the **School drive** will produce a weaker reward signal, and possibly a strong [incoherence penalty](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incoherence_penalty "Incoherence penalty"). The penalty will feed back to produce [reactance](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") against the [school drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_drive "School drive"), which will in turn require further coercive correction from the school system, which will in turn reduce the quality of knowledge further. Those feedback loops may lead to the dominance of one of the forces: the learn drive or the school drive. Thriving [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") increases [rebellion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") that increases defenses against the school drive. Similarly, increased penalization at school increases [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") that weakens the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") and results in submission to the system. Sadly, in most cases, the control system settles in the middle of those two extremes (see: the [old soup problem](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Old_soup_problem "Old soup problem")). Most children [hate school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Why_kids_hate_school%3F "Why kids hate school), lose their love of learning, and still submit to the enslavement. Their best chance for recovery is the freedom of college, or better yet, the freedom of adulthood. See: [Competitive feedback loops in binary decision making at neuronal level](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Competitive_feedback_loops_in_binary_decision_making_at_neuronal_level "Competitive feedback loops in binary decision making at neuronal level")*

Value de-conditioning via long-term depression will be the opposite to the process of building the network of [knowledge valuations](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation"). Knowledge devaluation will spread hierarchically via the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") in the same process that builds value of knowledge. Instead of [knowledge valuation network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation_network "Knowledge valuation network"), the same structure will form inversely oriented knowledge devaluation network (valuations go form goal to details of knowledge, while devaluations begin at the bottom conditioning out low-level interest with long-term depression and connection elimination spreading upwards in the hierarchy). The purpose of knowledge devaluation in the brain is to find the nearest valuation link that can be broken to eliminate the conflict.

Adaptation to school life is the reverse of the process of building hierarchical [knowledge valuations](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation") of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")

Broken links in the process of disuse may be removed permanently. If the conflict is violent, we may have [war of the networks](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/War_of_the_networks "War of the networks") with rapid changes to the [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network"). Those changes contribute to the claim that schools can induce fast changes. They do. Those changes consume neural substrate that might be used for building [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence").

Visible minor benefits of acceleration at school, hide invisible major losses in future learning capacity

When a student regains her freedom in college or in adult life, continual learning will lead to a recovery of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"). Passions and interests may return. However, the [conceptualization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptualization "Conceptualization") process proceeds largely in the direction of increased [memory stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Memory_stability "Memory stability"). This is why it is hard to reverse suppressed [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") after years of neglect. The old adage says: *"it is hard to teach an old dog new tricks"*. This true claim has little to do with the aging of the brain. It is largely based on the fact that the [conceptualization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptualization "Conceptualization") tends to crystallize the brain architecture, and all forms of negligence in youth are hard to reverse.

Due to the [conceptualization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Conceptualization "Conceptualization") process, devaluation of knowledge at school may impact the love of learning for life

## Complex world

The world may be getting more complex, but, from the point of an individual, it is getting easier to handle. This comes from the never ending progress in access to knowledge, the perpetual expansion of knowledge resources, and the never ending increase in the level of [abstractness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Abstractness "Abstractness") in which we can express complex notions. Human intelligence excels at perceiving complex reality using simple models.

The increase in the mass of knowledge is inconsequential. It does not prevent anyone from scanning Google's top 5 choices for the first insight into a concept. This is only going to get better. A child can learn about relativity theory today by choosing from thousands of resources that match her prior knowledge. The theory may be explained on models that are simpler than those that Einstein used in his reasoning. The equivalence principle can be explained as a simple ride in an elevator.

The increase in complexity is immaterial, if we experience improved observability and improved controllability. The world machine is getting more complex, but it gets relatively more sensors, and it is also rich in new actuators. Life is getting easier.

To preserve human [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") in the face of increasing complexity, we need to:

- foster freedom
- foster the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")
- emphasize [knowledge darwinism](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_darwinism "Knowledge darwinism") in learning (and in science)
- foster research

The idea that it is getting harder to handle the complex world may underlie some of the attempts to crank up the pressures of schooling. If current schooling is not enough, perhaps more schooling will help? This thinking about complexity may therefore be pretty harmful. Instead, we need to strive at better learning under the guidance of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive").

Complexity of the world is best tackled with rich [abstract knowledge](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Abstract_knowledge "Abstract knowledge")

## Research to the contrary

The harm of schooling is obvious to anyone who understand the roots of intelligence. It is obvious to anyone who studies history of great inventions or discoveries. If it is not obvious, it does not take much to ask kids in the nearby school about the intellectual impact of schooling.

However, the whole machinery of the [factory model of schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Factory_model_of_schooling "Factory model of schooling") would grind to a halt pretty fast if it did not have any backup in research. For taxpayer's money, researchers invent absurd metrics of learning, [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity"), and [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"). With a bit of data dredging, it does not take much to measure the benefits of schooling.

For example, researchers may claim that schools improve intelligence because IQ scores correlated with the number of years of schooling. They can also claim that grades correlate with intelligence. In the end, we may find out that improving grades improves a child's intelligence, which is patently absurd.

Grades may reflect subservience. They may also reflect innate smarts of students. [Cramming](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Cramming "Cramming") improves grades, however, it may also have a positive effect on IQ scores which are strongly based on skills drilled at school (3Rs)(see: [IQ is a dismal measure of intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/IQ_is_a_dismal_measure_of_intelligence "IQ is a dismal measure of intelligence")).

My own grades were excellent when my interests coincided with the demands of school. Most of the time though, my grades were awful. My IQ scores were great when I cared to perform. They were also awful at times (e.g. when taken experimentally in the middle of the night). In my case, correlations would be meaningless. The outcomes would be unpredictable. However, [peer review](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Peer_review "Peer review") and the publish-or-perish rule makes sure that there is a lot of research to back up the wishes of the regulators, who often claim that more schooling, means more education, which means better economy.

When you read that schools improve [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"), you should seriously re-examine the data, the sources, or the motives.

Schools that use any form of coercion are a serious threat to society's intelligence

The last people to notice the collapse will be the educators and teachers due to their [fish tank perspective](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Fish_tank_perspective "Fish tank perspective") and years of training along wrong models. [Concept networks](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") are perfectly adaptable, but their adaptability declines with knowledge [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability"). The old generation of educators is highly efficient at pushing in the wrong direction. They will be last to wake up (see: [Older people learn slower](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Older_people_learn_slower "Older people learn slower")).
