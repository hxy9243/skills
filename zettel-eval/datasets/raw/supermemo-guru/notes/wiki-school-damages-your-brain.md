If you look for [peer-reviewed](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Peer-reviewed "Peer-reviewed") research showing that [coercive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_schooling "Coercive schooling") results in brain injury or brain damage, you will not be successful. You need to connect the dots or rely on experts in the field.

The state of research on the effect of schooling on the brain is comparable to that of the research on the harm of cigarette smoke from a century ago. Everyone seems to know some aspect of the problem, but it is hard to set up an experiment that will definitively prove the claim of injury.

The control sample should be taken from the best research population. Such a population would be made of students of [democratic schools](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Democratic_school "Democratic school") and [unschoolers](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Unschooler "Unschooler"). To date, it is even hard to measure the educational outcomes of freedom, let alone the benefits of freedom for brain health and mental well-being.

This text is an attempt to shortly synthesize (1) the research in psychology and psychiatry, and (2) the research in brain science (at the synaptic and architectural level).

## Schools cause maladaptation

The brain responds to experience by adaptation. Adaptation occurs via synaptic plasticity. Long-term adaptation changes the architecture of the brain.

Children who are subject to coercive schooling from young ages to adulthood arrive at architectures that are largely maladaptive. The reason for that maladaptation is the fact that schools form an artificial environment that does not even remotely reflect the environments in which the adult brain will need to operate.

As a result, there is little exaggeration in the following claim:

Long years of coercive schoolings in childhood result in **brain damage**

**Brain damage** may sound drastic. After all, the whole world employs [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling") that involves coercion. Adults love their children. How could they possibly agree to damage their brains? The sole reason is ignorance. Experts know it. The public is unconcerned.

Brains damaged by school lose the most essential property that underlies life-long learning, [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity") and [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"): **the love of learning** (see: [Pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning")). This undermines individual's chances in adult life. This also has serious consequences for mental health. This can be worse than physical trauma to the brain. > ***Figure:** This is how school destroys the love of learning. **[Learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")** is the set of passions and interests that a child would like to pursue. **[School drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_drive "School drive")** is the set of rewards and penalties set up by the school system. **Learn drive** leads to simple, mnemonic, [coherent](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherent "Coherent"), [stable](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stable "Stable") and [applicable](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Applicable "Applicable") memories due to the fact that the quality of knowledge determines the degree of reward in the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") system. **School drive** leads to complex, short-term memories vulnerable to [interference](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Interference "Interference") due to the fact that schools serialize knowledge by [curriculum](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curriculum "Curriculum") (not by the neural mechanism of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")). [Competitive inhibition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/War_of_the_networks "War of the networks") between the **Learn drive** and the **School drive** circuits will lead to the weakening of neural connections. Strong **School drive** will weaken the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), destroy the passion for learning, and lead to [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"). Powerful **Learn drive** will lead to [rebellion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") that will protect intrinsic passions, but possibly will also lead to problems at school. Storing new knowledge under the influence of **Learn drive** is highly rewarding and carries no penalty (by definition of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")). This will make the learn drive thrive leading to success in learning (and at school). In contrast, poor quality of knowledge induced by the pressures of the **School drive** will produce a weaker reward signal, and possibly a strong [incoherence penalty](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incoherence_penalty "Incoherence penalty"). The penalty will feed back to produce [reactance](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") against the [school drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/School_drive "School drive"), which will in turn require further coercive correction from the school system, which will in turn reduce the quality of knowledge further. Those feedback loops may lead to the dominance of one of the forces: the learn drive or the school drive. Thriving [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") increases [rebellion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") that increases defenses against the school drive. Similarly, increased penalization at school increases [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") that weakens the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") and results in submission to the system. Sadly, in most cases, the control system settles in the middle of those two extremes (see: the [old soup problem](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Old_soup_problem "Old soup problem")). Most children [hate school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Why_kids_hate_school%3F "Why kids hate school), lose their love of learning, and still submit to the enslavement. Their best chance for recovery is the freedom of college, or better yet, the freedom of adulthood. See: [Competitive feedback loops in binary decision making at neuronal level](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Competitive_feedback_loops_in_binary_decision_making_at_neuronal_level "Competitive feedback loops in binary decision making at neuronal level")*

## Mechanism of the harm

Here is the shortest possible line of reasoning that explains the brain damage caused by coercive schooling:

- [coercive learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_learning "Coercive learning") requires redirecting of the brain resources from knowledge considered worth-knowing as determined by the [knowledge valuation network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation_network "Knowledge valuation network"), to knowledge that is considered worth-knowing by external actors (parents, teachers, etc.)(see: [Optimality of the learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Optimality_of_the_learn_drive "Optimality of the learn drive"))
- coercive redirects result in conditioning that suppresses [knowledge valuation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation") signals via the mechanisms dubbed the **[war of the networks](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/War_of_the_networks "War of the networks")**
- as a result of coercion, [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") develops. It can lead to depression and a whole spectrum of mental health problems, incl. [addictions](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Addiction "Addiction")
- [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") results in less learning, less [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity"), lesser capacity to [solve problems](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving"), lesser ability to adapt to new environment, and less [resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resilience "Resilience")

In simplest terms, students accustomed to being controlled during their formative years, lose their ability to autonomously adapt to new environments by means of creative learning.

Control via coercion undermines autonomy, [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence"), and [resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resilience "Resilience")

## Diagrams

The following diagrams show the mechanism of harm. You can track it up from (1) the simplest organism with just 300 neurons, via (2) competitive feedback loops in simplest neural circuits to (3) the macroscopic view as observed by students at school:

![](https://supermemo.guru/images/thumb/e/ed/Curiosity%2C_creativity_and_learned_helplessness_in_a_worm.png/500px-Curiosity%2C_creativity_and_learned_helplessness_in_a_worm.png)

> ***Figure:** C. elegans has a nervous system made of only 302 neurons. However, this is enough to implement an [exploratory algorithm](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Exploratory_algorithm "Exploratory algorithm") that is reminiscent of human [curiosity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curiosity "Curiosity"), [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity"), and [problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving"). When the worm finds a patch of food, it will explore it. However, on occasion it will take an unexpected dash in a random direction in search of new patches (bacteria). Similar algorithms can be found in other animals, however, human [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") is far more complex. It is based on [knowledge valuation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation") and the exploratory breaks are reserved for period of [learntropy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learntropy "Learntropy") dropping well below the expected value. Human creativity emerges from complex cognitive processes informed by knowledge, in contrast to the worm's behavior, which consists solely of stochastic directional choices. For the worm, a new patch of bacteria is a problem solved, for a human it might be a new idea for terraforming Mars. Last but not least, the metaphoric tool for inducing [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") (marked as "school") in primitive animals will rather only have the form of drive habituation. Nevertheless, the little worm may present a convincing illustration that the [intelligent missile metaphor](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligent_missile_metaphor "Intelligent missile metaphor") is far more universal and may be relevant to primitive nervous systems as well.* For more on the universality of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") see: [The psychology and neuroscience of curiosity](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4635443)

![](https://supermemo.guru/images/thumb/2/22/Competitive_feedback_loops_in_binary_decision_making_at_neuronal_level.png/500px-Competitive_feedback_loops_in_binary_decision_making_at_neuronal_level.png)

Inhibitory neurons **IA** and **IB** decrease the chance of firing in the competitive decision neuron (**Decision A** or **Decision B**). History of prior decisions will determine synaptic [stabilization](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stabilization "Stabilization"), which will favor decisions that used to bring higher past rewards. > - Knowledge in blue is valued as of suboptimum quality and relevance (see: [complexity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Complexity "Complexity"), [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence"), [applicability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Applicability "Applicability"), [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability"))

> - Due to their impact on the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), penalties in learning lead to a form of [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") manifested as the loss of the [pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning")
> - [Reward deficit](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Reward_deficit "Reward deficit") in [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") increases the risk of addictions, depression, and other mental disorders (see: [Reward diversity in preventing addictions](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Reward_diversity_in_preventing_addictions "Reward diversity in preventing addictions"))
> - [Extrinsic motivation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Extrinsic_motivation "Extrinsic motivation") (incl. rewards for learning) makes it possible to encode unwanted memories (with loss of [coherence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coherence "Coherence"), [stability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stability "Stability"), [applicability](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Applicability "Applicability"), and more)
> - [Extrinsic motivation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Extrinsic_motivation "Extrinsic motivation") results in the override of the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"), suppresses encoding of wanted memories, and contributes to [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness") through the effect on wanting and liking

## Research

If you attempt to search for peer-reviewed publications on brain damage caused by coercion, you will find none. If one popped up, it would be blasted around the world by the media. [Peer-reviewed science](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Peer_review "Peer review") cannot easily connect these simple dots. There is no good way to measure the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"). [Knowledge acquisition curve](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_acquisition_curve "Knowledge acquisition curve") in [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo") is a good indicator as long as it is not coerced. Changes to brain topology are not easily identified by neuro-imaging methodology. Longitudinal study would probably need to take at least a decade. However, George Land attempted to measure [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity") and noticed a drastic drop at the age of school entry. [Peter Gray](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Peter_Gray "Peter Gray") and others observed a dramatic increase in mental health problems at the start of the school year. Addictions that stem from coercion are seen all around, however, they are wrongly blamed on addictive agents (e.g. alcohol or computer games), or on students themselves. All teachers will tell you that *" [teenagers are lazy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Myth:_Students_are_naturally_lazy_and_do_not_like_to_learn "Myth: Students are naturally lazy and do not like to learn") "*, which is a reflection of the loss of the love of learning. It is also possible to list individual bad habits that develop as a result of schooling. See: [100 bad habits learned at school](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/100_bad_habits_learned_at_school "100 bad habits learned at school"). All dots are there, but experts able to connect them are yet to write about it boldly in respectable journals devoted to neuroscience.

Why so many people say they hate math or Shakespeare?

We are a damaged society of damaged brains!
