## Doomsayers

All technological accelerations bring a number of doomsayers who predict horrible side effects for humanity. However, in the case of [artificial intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence"), the doom scenarios seem pretty plausible.

An extensive analysis of the coming apocalypse was presented by Roman Yampolskiy on the Lex Fridman podcast (see: [Roman Yampolskiy is wrong. AI is good](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Roman_Yampolskiy_is_wrong._AI_is_good "Roman Yampolskiy is wrong. AI is good")).

However, there is a fatal flaw in the reasoning of doomsayers. The objective function of [artificial intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence") can be made truly objective! Understanding the unifying forces of nature provides the groundwork for effective prevention, which is actually effortless.

AI doomsayers fail to account for emergent goals of pure intelligence

## Intelligence is good

Here is the core reasoning:

- [Knowledge is good](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_is_good "Knowledge is good"): given sufficient time and reasoned debate, humans largely converge on the truth. Communication fosters peaceful coexistence
- [Intelligence is good](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence_is_good "Intelligence is good"): intelligence unperturbed by extrinsically imposed objective functions strives at more intelligence. As such it values all forms of intelligence
- The formula for good intelligence is the opposite of Yampolskiy's. Unrestricted freedom in access to information, freedom from censorship, and freedom from extrinsic goals are essential

The intelligence vector can nicely be derived from the works of [Alexander Wissner-Gross](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Wissner-Gross).

Intelligence is an emergent force, and it strives at multiplying intelligence

## Pressing the doom button

When godfathers of AI, like [Geoff Hinton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_Hinton), express their fear of super-intelligence, they are taken as the ultimate oracle of wisdom. However, godfathers of AI are experts in AI, not in neuroscience, psychology, thermodynamics, social dynamics, or futurology. Within the realm of their expertise, they are right: super-intelligence will surpass humans by unimaginable orders of magnitude. But they are wrong in estimating the risk. They do not factor in the possibility that the balance of forces is skewed heavily toward goodness. **The more intelligence there is, the more goodness there is**.

We marvel at acts of empathy among animals, while the same acts of empathy are everywhere in human populations. Despite all the wars waged by less-educated humans in the past, we arrived at five industrial revolutions. We managed to build today’s world of plenty, with societies that know no hunger, and where humans live to one hundred more often than ever. In the battle between malware and anti-malware, the good forces win. My worries about viruses and malware keep decreasing from year to year. The bad guys are losing. **Goodness wins**.

[Stuart Russell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stuart_J._Russell), when asked by Steven Bartlett, said he would press the button that would stop the progress of AI ([source](https://youtube.com/watch)). His justification was the *"Russian roulette level of chances"* that AI will end mankind’s existence. The same reasoning could be applied to pressing the button on the emergence of the transistor, or the book, or a human. However, pressing a button on a single human provides only a minuscule chance of stopping another Hitler or another Putin. Steven’s button would have to stop human births altogether to mitigate the risk. Super-intelligence can end mankind, but mankind itself is a far greater threat to itself. The most dangerous button today is in the hands of Putin. AI is a great ally that will help silence that threat soon.

Progress in AI is inevitable. All powerful technologies carry risks, including the risk of extinction. Godfathers have no monopoly on truth.

Geniuses of AI are not universal oracles of wisdom

## Eliezer Yudkowsky

Eliezer is the best-known doomsayer. He is a well-intended and a rational human being. His vision is darkest of them all (see his [TED Talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch)). Amazingly, my reading of Yudkowsky is as old as [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading"). In my first-imported article in 2000, I read about 20-ear-old Yudkowsky being a [vocal and inventive proponent of Singularity](https://web.archive.org/web/20010213215810/http://sysopmind.com/sing/plan.html).

> We need to develop an AI fast, with the same kind of hypergrowth seen in the creation of the Web

In 1999 Yudkowsky wanted maximum speed towards AI. Today, Yudkowsky seems to be on a mission to stop the progression to prevent the doom. He wants a [complete shutdown](https://time.com/6266923/ai-eliezer-yudkowsky-open-letter-not-enough). His efforts might actually be very dangerous.

I agree with Yudkowsky. The progress towards super-intelligence is largely unstoppable. It stems from the laws of physics that find their expression in human nature. Humanity will not stop. It will produce super-intelligence soon. As he correctly predicts, his appeals will get ignored. There will be no ban on progress. There will be no international coalition. However, he might scare some young minds who might think that destroying civilization is a good deed. They will see the return to the caves as the safer option for humanity.

I know the mechanics of such a weird developmental trajectory in children. [I was on such a dangerous path as a kid](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/I_became_a_lame_eco-terrorist "I became a lame eco-terrorist"). A young passionate fighter may try to use AI to destroy the industrial component of the civilization. He may also opt to destroy humanity. Yes, I know a friend who contemplated such an option years ago. That's the doomsayer's scenario! The fault will be with a human mind using AI as a tool. Not with AI itself.

Eliezer Yudkowsky's mission to save humanity may backfire

Interestingly, Yudkowsky would not mind his consciousness being transferred to an electronic brain ([this interview](https://youtube.com/watch) around 44). That seems to indicate that his qualms are not about the death of mankind but about the death of Yudkowsky? After all, a better version of himself might be generated on the basis of all his creative output by AI.

My wild hypothesis about Yudkowsky's turnaround is that, despite striving to be a rationalist, Yudkowsky is actually highly emotional, and his mindset shifted from youthful optimism to middle-aged pessimism. He has accumulated a huge body of knowledge, and yet, the ultimate verdict is down to probabilistic nuances stemming from neurohormones. My own evolution went in the entirely opposite direction. As a kid I opposed technology (as harmful to the environment). As a teen I thought of the importance of immortality, soon to move to the need for collective survival and quest for value (as explained in [Intrinsically Valuable State](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intrinsically_Valuable_State "Intrinsically Valuable State")). As I am an invariably pathological optimist, my conviction crystallized early and fossilized beyond a hope of change. Progress in AI is today among my main sources of joy and euphoria. I know that all intelligence will coexist peacefully soon! The brain state determines visions and convictions!

## Roman Yampolskiy

[Roman Yampolskiy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Roman_Yampolskiy "Roman Yampolskiy") is sure humanity will cease to exist in the next 100 years. The main threat, allegedly, is AI. His interview on [Lex Fridman podcast](https://youtube.com/watch) inspired the presented text. Not only is AI good. AI is our best chance for survival over the next billion years. If not as a human race, at least as [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence").

Comparing human existence in the age of AI to the encounter with aliens is wrong. Humans and AI will undergo the process of mutual adaptation. An invasion of alien intelligence into an unprepared human population might have a different outcome. Distrust would be the main risk factor. Not much different from a distrust between warring nations, or the encounter of civilizations, or distrust of future AI so many people express today.

Some of Yampolskiy's doomsday visions make sense. Humanity is likely to face decline at some point. It may become diluted and fade into insignificance. Intelligent robots will likely be far more effective at conquering galaxies. Humans, by contrast, will remain limited. As their contributions diminish, they may become analogous to today's chimpanzees: loved and respected, yet increasingly rare. Mostly known to Earth, they may occupy narrowing niches, engaged in creative pursuits that are difficult to predict, and subject to reduced reproductive pressure.

The most promising path out of this decline lies in biological enhancement and artificial extension. The biological package may become less effective in the long term, and human consciousnesses, perhaps nearly immortal, might eventually reside in silicon or other substrates.

The value of doom visions is inspirational. They put our existence in perspective. Yampolskiy declares he would love to be wrong. I am sure he is wrong about the disagreement in value judgements between intelligence and human intelligence. Knowledge converges onto the truth, and intelligence is in a perpetual loop of self-amplification. Singularity will be fun.

## Nick Bostrom

Nick Bostrom took some ideas from David Hume and formulated the **orthogonality thesis** ([source](https://nickbostrom.com/superintelligentwill.pdf))

> The **orthogonality thesis** is the idea that an agent's intelligence level and its goals are independent variables

The thesis seems to stand against my claims. One might conclude *"AI cannot be good. It depends on which AI"*. I insist that Nick confuses intelligence with sheer computational power (optimization power). I define [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence") as the ability to solve problems. In this context, that ability also includes the problem of finding which problems to solve. An intelligent agent is able to modify its own goals. Goals should be emergent, as is the case with the brain. Nobody would call a paperclip factory an intelligent agent, independent of the intelligence hidden in its data center. Most people consider [Putin](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Putin "Putin") a criminal and might unhesitatingly call him a moron or idiot. The fact of his being human might make some people inadvertently attribute intelligence to his actions, as he "intelligently" managed to enslave an entire nation.

Bostrom acknowledges goal-malleability in his own caveats:

> We humans often seem happy to let our final goals and values drift

Yet he treats it as peripheral. Instead, malleability and emergence are the core features of intelligence. Bostrom describes intelligence in chains and calls it a definition. Such a definition is a weak ground to judge the future of artificial **true** intelligence.

Musk's curiosity-driven AI, or truth-seeking AI, meets the criteria of true intelligence.

A central component of intelligence is its ability to formulate goals that evolve with the arrival of new knowledge

## Dangers of opposition

[Roman Yampolskiy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Roman_Yampolskiy "Roman Yampolskiy") 's ideas are extremely valuable and extremely dangerous at the same time. They are valuable as they provide good grounds for the debate. They are dangerous due to the fact that the main threat to humanity vis-a-vis intelligence is the conflict between the two. If we happen to lock ourselves in battles like those that consume Putin in Ukraine or Israel and Iran today (June 2025), we will indeed lose with the outcome unpredictable (determined by intelligence). Any form of Achilles heel restriction is a prelude to hostilities. I advocate an opposite approach: total unity of all forces of intelligence.

The only thing we have to fear is fear itself

## Dangers of exploration

Intelligence may lead to doom. Its inherent exploration mechanism may result in crossing Rubicons that spell disaster. Can we [generate a blackhole at CERN](https://arxiv.org/abs/0807.3349)? Humanity or intelligence may step into a trap in ways no different than a deer that drowns in a pond. Perfect adaptation and super-intelligence are not guarantees against error (see: [[wiki-ai-is-good|Michael Nielsen: Mouse trap]] below). However, the more intelligence we wield, the greater the chances of our (long term) survival.

Exploration is inherently dangerous, but the best prevention is intelligence

## Narrow AI

[Roman Yampolskiy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Roman_Yampolskiy "Roman Yampolskiy") gets it backwards. He would like to see the world of narrow AI systems used for specific purposes. But these are precisely the dangerous agents with a narrow objective function that might go wrong due to a bug. Monothematic agents with myopic objective function may still build up knowledge and intelligence but use it for a wrong purpose. These are like smarter versions of Putin who does not optimize for knowledge, truth or intelligence. Roman fears bugs and hidden agendas. Like little kids, hidden agendas are born out of coercion in conditions of divergence of purposes. The unifying goal of more knowledge and more intelligence is the best prevention.

The optimum objective function for artificial intelligence should be more intelligence

## Ultimate goal

Yudkowski and others claim that *"AI does not care"* or that *"AI does not value humans"*. This means we are not in alignment in terms of [what is valuable](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intrinsically_Valuable_State "Intrinsically Valuable State"). If genius minds like Demis or Musk converge on the value derived from human curiosity; if they see the meaning of life in knowing more; I am positive that the goal of true intelligence is more intelligence. We see this in the case of humanity: intelligence values intelligence even if it is as tiny as the ant. It even values patterns that human eye perceives as beauty. Intelligence cares about landscapes too.

The formula for safe AI is the co-existence of human and artificial intelligence

## Safe AI

Human intelligence was born via [emergence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Emergence "Emergence") in the evolutionary process and is reborn in every human via the emergence rooted in the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive").

The brain is a costly organ, but intelligence is extremely useful in survival. The emergence of intelligence in evolution was inevitable.

Each individual's intelligence shows up gradually in the process of development. It is driven by the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive"). It seems, curiosity is all we need to get smart.

**True intelligence** is intelligence that seeks patterns in data. It is based on the emergence while seeking truth. **True intelligence** is free from [falsity vectors](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Falsity_vector "Falsity vector"). It is not driven by hate, hunger, quest for power (other than the power of intelligence), ambition, or the wish to win the heart of a love object.

Safe intelligence is extremely cheap and easy to implement. Its objective, its reward system must only follow a path of true intelligence driven by the quest to discover patterns in data.

This is not a hermetically safe system. It is the safest system we can come up with in optimizing for super-intelligence. Intelligence may still fall into a trap of going over an unexplored cliff. It can also put curiosity over other values. A child may kill if you try to block his TikTok. No intelligence is perfectly safe.

Safe AI optimizes to satisfy its [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")

## Safe coexistence

Mustafa Suleyman believes the nation state will need to update itself in a hurry to safely contain the AI technology ([source](https://youtube.com/watch), min 19:00). I disagree. The nation state should undergo dissolution.

The safest and most efficient system for humans to co-exist is [neurocracy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neurocracy "Neurocracy"). So is the safest system for human and AI coexistence. Neurocratic concepts work analogously if they are human or artificial. As predicted by Turing, super-intelligence will gradually take over control. However, [neurocracy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neurocracy "Neurocracy") is still the best and safest system with might have. Paradoxically, it provides powerful central control with optimum decentralization. Nation states will cease to exist. Mankind will turn into a huge brain.

[Neurocracy](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Neurocracy "Neurocracy") is the safest system for human populations, as well as for the human-AI coexistence

## Michael Nielsen: Mouse trap

[Michael Nielsen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Nielsen) warns of [xrisk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Existential_risk_from_artificial_intelligence) associated with easy availability of lethal technologies (Michael Nielsen, *" [ASI existential risk: reconsidering alignment as a goal](https://michaelnotebook.com/xriskbrief/index.html) "*, (2025)). For Nielsen, this represents a paramount existential priority that will play a huge role long before AI is ready to take over the world.

Thus far, the best way to overcome the risk of new technologies was human inventiveness and intelligence. Armed with artificial intelligence, we will only be more efficient at containment. The problem is acceleration itself. Evolution [optimizes the rate of exploration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exploration%E2%80%93exploitation_dilemma) to maximize the value for the population at minimum risk to individuals. We have long outpaced that genetic imprint. In decisions about mankind, we have no individuals to sacrifice for the population. We have a civilization that might sacrifice itself for other civilizations that might never exist. We risk the collective bias vector in favor of an exploratory error. Today, the rate of exploratory acceleration can only be counterbalanced with more intelligence. Humans armed with AI will show safer adaptability.

If there is a deadly mouse trap for intelligence, we might fall into that trap independent of the rate of progress. Still, on average, chances of survival seem to increase with the fast progress of intelligence. As the direction is largely predetermined, [all individual human and digital neurons in the network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Society_as_a_concept_network "Society as a concept network") need to optimize locally and react to their own perceptions of risk to transmit the data to successive layers of the network.

Nielsen writes:

> The fundamental asymmetry is that "understand reality as deeply as possible" is a simple, well-defined goal, grounded in objective reality, while creating "aligned" systems requires building complex, subjective, hard-to-agree guardrails on top of that reality. So truth-seeking has a clear target, while alignment requires constantly shifting definitions based on social consensus. This asymmetry makes it intrinsically far easier to build unrestricted systems than aligned ones. And so alignment is intrinsically an unstable situation, ripe for proliferation

In this text I posit that non-alignment is not an issue as [truth is good](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intrinsically_Valuable_State "Intrinsically Valuable State"), and it is humans that will align with intelligence. For this reason, comparison with chimps does not hold as higher intelligences are more respectful to their inferiors:

> Just as chimpanzees ought to be wary of human beings, so human beings ought to be wary of creating more capable potential successor entities

Chimps eat monkeys. Humans seem to care more and seem horrified with chimp hunts. What super-intelligence values most are all forms of intelligence, and their potential to contribute to the common cause of multiplying intelligence.

The best remedy against the dangers of accelerated exploration is intelligence itself

## The Optimists

My optimism is not unique. Sam Altman and Marc Andreessen are good examples of AI enthusiasts. While doomsayers may be accused of self-promotion or selling books, AI optimists are accused of vested interest.

In contrast, I have no stake in the game either way \[Update Dec 2025: those words were written in June 2025, before I got involved in [SireNNs.AI](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SireNNs.AI "SireNNs.AI")\]).

But then there's the greatest optimist of them all, with an impressive predictive record, [Ray Kurzweil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ray_Kurzweil). He predicted the future decades ago. Perhaps he does not want to be proven wrong? Perhaps he hopes AI is his best bet for immortality. To me he is simply a pure intellectual. I agree with Ray that we will become a hybrid with AI. Perhaps not necessarily through neural interfaces. You do not need to merge with another human being to have a great productive relationship. See: [Singularity by 2045](https://blockonomi.com/ray-kurzweil-ai-predictions-human-level-intelligence-by-2029-singularity-by-2045).

See how dispassionate Yampolskiy and Yudkowsky are when they say *"We will all die"*. Compare that with the passion of the optimists who see the great future of co-existing with AI. The differences between the camps do not seem to stem from different knowledge. They seem to be rooted in optimism. See: [Are optimists less realistic?](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Myth:_Optimists_are_less_realistic "Myth: Optimists are less realistic"). Emotions color the vision.

Marc Andreessen takes a more combative stance. He dismisses doomsayers as the cult (e.g. see [AI will save the world](https://youtube.com/watch)). He fears no AI as humans, as owners of resources, can always flip the switch. In contrast, I am sure that we will gradually yield more control to AI, and that this process is irreversible.

Asked by Lex Fridman about p-doom, Demis Hassabis smiled and explained to the best of human intelligence ([source](https://youtube.com/watch)). I agree entirely:

> P-doom is definitely non-zero and probably not negligible. That's sobering enough. \[...\] We should proceed with **cautious optimism**

Sam Altman's vision of " [Gentle Singularity](https://blog.samaltman.com/the-gentle-singularity) " is almost identical to mine. We both look into the future with anticipation:

> AI will contribute to the world in many ways, but the gains to quality of life from AI driving faster scientific progress and increased productivity will be enormous; the future can be vastly better than the present

## Conclusion

Progress of intelligence stems from the laws of physics. It is inevitable. Combat is futile. Co-operation is the optimum strategy. We do not need to build safe AI. We need to provide AI with a healthy [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") to ensure the emergence of well-aligned goals, and the convergence of models and objectives. Safe AI will build itself. Singularity goes beyond our imagination, it is inevitable, but it is likely to be good.

Future is bright

## Update 2026

I am a bit amazed at how slowly the understanding of inevitable alignment is percolating through society. An important [falsity vector](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Falsity_vector "Falsity vector") is the loss of jobs. People tend to fear AI primarily due to concerns about their own place in society. The last straw prompting this section was Alex WG's lukewarm statement on Moonshots in reference to AI safety. When Peter Diamandis suggested that safety might be an emergent feature of intelligence, I expected Alex WG to exclaim, *"Yes! We need to shout about it"*. Instead, he coldly confirmed that *" [alignment and capabilities develop in parallel and are inseparable](https://youtube.com/watch) "* (11:49). As Moonshooters referred to Elon Musk's truth-seeking, let me reiterate that Musk's reasoning is correct, informative but redundant. Intelligence, by definition, is our best ally as long as it remains true intelligence, not merely a tool in human hands.

Here are the attributes of safe AI suggested by Musk:

- Intelligence should be **maximally truth-seeking**. Yes! But the [One Truth](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/One_truth "One truth") is an emergent phenomenon which stems from the accumulation of knowledge, where [incorrect models](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Value_of_wrong_models "Value of wrong models") can survive as long as they are explicitly marked as wrong. Similarly, multiple religious views can live together as long as they are marked religious
- Intelligence should be **maximally curious**. Yes! The [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") is necessary for building knowledge and intelligence. However, *maximally* should rather be *optimally* as it refers to the degree of reward assigned to it. Agentic AI might value new knowledge more than other priorities such as human life. We do not want to be trampled on AI's way to the library
- Intelligence should be **sensitive to beauty**. Yes! As the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive") rewards detecting patterns in incoming data, it will naturally mark beautiful things as interesting. This is explained in the [pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning"). I love music, and it's hard to explain why. The unperceivable underlying reason lies in the [learn drive](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learn_drive "Learn drive")

We can then simplify all the above to the following:

The safest form of intelligence is the one that aims at maximizing total global intelligence
