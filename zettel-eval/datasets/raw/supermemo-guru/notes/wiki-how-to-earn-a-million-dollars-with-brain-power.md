## Going for a Guiness Record

[Spaced repetition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition") is four decades old. As soon as we set up a [commercial company](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo_World "SuperMemo World"), we had a dream to achieve some great memory feats with spaced repetition to showcase it to the world. We groomed many young people for the job. The prime goal was the Guiness Record in knowledge of English vocabulary. It would combine a great flashy achievement with some useful knowledge for the record holder. The usual procedure was to memorize [Advanced English](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Advanced_English "Advanced English") in a few months (40,000+ [items](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Item "Item")). Afair, someone crammed it in a month, probably with horrible retention ([measured forgetting index](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Measured_forgetting_index "Measured forgetting index")). However, as soon as one had to start adding his own [items](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Item "Item"), the number of [repetitions](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Repetition "Repetition") became a burden that would discourage going beyond 50,000 items. After a few years of trying with a dozen candidates, we gave up. I was myself tempted to achieve some spectacular goal, but life was too busy in the 1990s.

## We hit the million at last

Three decades later, I have just received a heart-warming message from [Jonas von Essen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonas_von_Essen) with a sweet *"thank you for spaced repetition"* (Jonas used Anki to memorize his cards). I was very happy that Jonas's thank you came almost immediately after his great achievement. He won *" [Who wants to be a millionaire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Be_a_Millionaire%3F) "* (Swedish edition).

![](https://supermemo.guru/images/thumb/9/9a/Jonas_von_Essen.jpg/400px-Jonas_von_Essen.jpg)

Jonas was never groomed. He achieved everything on his own steam. I learned about his achievement directly from him after the fact.

The conclusion is that it is not the technology that leads to success. It is the personality, and perseverance that stems from [passion](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Passion "Passion"). The same precious passion that so many people lose at school, and which is not easy to recover. With a wrong schooled approach, [spaced repetition](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spaced_repetition "Spaced repetition") might actually make learning worse! See: [Hating SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Hating_SuperMemo "Hating SuperMemo").

If you now think that Jonas has just shown you your path to a million, you might be wrong: Jonas is a special case. He had already won Memory Championships twice. He was very unhappy with his world record in memorizing Pi (he slipped much earlier than he did in training). The feat of reproducing Pi seems suitable only for computers, but Jonas aimed at recalling 100,000 digits. This explains the degree of his passion and his ambition. He explained *"I just confused two items on a peg list"*.

To earn your million, instead for going for competitions, go for a specific creative goal that nobody was able to achieve. A goal that many people need, and few even know it exists. Chances of replicating Jonas's success are negligible. Spaced repetition is no magic. This is hard work.

## Jonas and memory

Today, Jonas uses Anki, and he still almost instinctively uses mnemonics when formulating items. I think, in the ideal case, the conscious effort should entirely leave the equation (which requires religious obeyance of [20 rules](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/20_rules "20 rules")). Jonas is not entirely happy with his estimated [forgetting index](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Forgetting_index "Forgetting index") of 10%. If you use SuperMemo and have similar feelings, remember that pesky SuperMemo will increase intervals for you to hit exactly 10% so that to increase the [memory effect](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Spacing_effect "Spacing effect"). It is possible to reduce the forgetting index (as a setting for a [collection](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Collection "Collection") or for individual [items](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Item "Item")), but it is not recommended. 10% established itself as a golden mean, while higher forgetting may actually increase the [speed of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acquisition_rate "Acquisition rate").

Jonas enjoys his repetitions. If it is not contents, then it is the goals that make his learning happy most of the time. However, he considers himself well disciplined, and can pull extra effort in times of need.

If I was to advice about your million with [SuperMemo](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/SuperMemo "SuperMemo"), I would say: start small, add a few life-changing [items](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Item "Item"), and make it an enjoyable habit (see: [Pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning"). Over time you will likely advance to [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading"). This should happen only when you feel the burning need or when your curiosity peaks. Never push against your inner voice, which tells you *"too much"*.

Congratulations Jonas. You lift spirits in the entire spaced repetition community!
