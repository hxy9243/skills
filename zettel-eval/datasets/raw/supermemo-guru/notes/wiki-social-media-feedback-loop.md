There is a great deal of worry about the impact of social media on youth. Few people realize that the actual cause of trouble is [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling"). Without coercion in the equation, social media is actually a human dream come true: a happiness pill. To turn the villan into a hero, we only need to understand the mechanisms of its interaction with the human brain. This interaction is based on a set of feedback loops that can equally lead to euphoria or to depression. It can even trigger bipolar oscillations.

For a healthy mind, social media is a source of great pleasure and a great deal of high-quality learning.

Human [curiosity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Curiosity "Curiosity") picks interesting information from the environment. It then matches it with [knowledge in the brain](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Prior_knowledge "Prior knowledge") and helps remember things of [highest value](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation"). **[Exploratory learning algorithm](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Exploratory_learning_algorithm "Exploratory learning algorithm")** is responsible for selecting best channels and the best pieces of knowledge.

Social media uses algorithms that dramatically enhance this search for golden knowledge. Using [AI](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/AI "AI"), the algorithms build a model of user's interests. This model is a reflection of the model of the world built as a [concept network](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Concept_network "Concept network") in user's brain. Digital models used on the web are simpler, biased for the available database (e.g. that of YouTube videos), and slow to discover interests that grow outside the human-to-digital connection. Otherwise, the two world models grow in synchrony: in the brain and in the digital system.

The brain and its reflection in the digital world build synergistic models of reality in synchronous mutual feedback

Like a human brain, social media model can undergo subset activations that represent current interests (see: [mental computation](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Mental_computation "Mental computation")). Someone may be interested in medical sciences, but on a given day, she may be focused on Parkinson's disease, e.g. when inspired by the [Biden-Trump debate](https://www.vox.com/politics/359914/biden-parkinsons-president-parkinsonism-neurolog).

While humans select knowledge channels, and pick pieces by streaming knowledge (e.g. from YouTube) or [speed-reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Speed-reading "Speed-reading") posts (e.g. from Facebook), AI can instantly search vast databases of available knowledge and instantly bring to front a selection that maximizes a match with current interests. On a day of browsing materials on Parkinson's, YouTube will make intelligent guesses as to what relevant videos should be recommended.

**[Exploratory learning algorithm](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Exploratory_learning_algorithm "Exploratory learning algorithm")** and social media algorithms live in a mutual feedback loop that maximizes reward, which results in the [pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning"). The degree of perfection in both algorithms is high enough to justify the term **Educational Singularity**. The consumption of knowledge proceeds at light speed today. It is limited only by the bottlenecks of human [long-term memory](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Long-term_memory "Long-term memory"). Even those bottlenecks get widened by high [knowledge valuations](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Knowledge_valuation "Knowledge valuation") that facilitates long-term [retention](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Retention "Retention"). With a bit of help from [incremental reading](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_reading "Incremental reading") or [incremental video](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Incremental_video "Incremental video"), we can say we are probably hovering at the **maximum biologically possible speed of learning**.

Do we need further improvement? We are already streaming knowledge into our heads at incredible speeds. Human [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity") that results from the acquired knowledge is also soaring. It is the consumption/exploitation of the new ideas that becomes a "problem" of its own. One might risk a claim: **new ideas are cheap now**. Bringing them to life is the hard part.

## Elation or despair

Positive feedback loop of the algorithms maximizes the [pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning"). However, it is true that social media may also bring distress and trauma. The latter may occasionally result from natural variability of human fortunes. Life is a streak of successes and failures. So is one's existence in social media. Mixed fortunes are a natural component of [problem solving](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Problem_solving "Problem solving").

A far larger problem though is the loss of [resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resilience "Resilience") and poor ability to handle [acute stress](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Acute_stress "Acute stress") in modern populations. As a result of [coercive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_schooling "Coercive schooling"), young population is perpetually pushed in the direction of [learned helplessness](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Learned_helplessness "Learned helplessness"), which in turn disrupts the balance of rewards and penalties in the brain. Coercive school is an invention that breeds adaptation to non-adaptation. In other words, being [well-schooled](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Well-schooled "Well-schooled") but inflexible in new learning is the harmful outcome of [compulsory schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Compulsory_schooling "Compulsory schooling") (see: [Missile metaphor](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Missile_metaphor "Missile metaphor")). School kills [intelligence](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Intelligence "Intelligence").

In the optimum healthy state, sleep resets the reward system so that we wake up in a neutral contented state. When the first activations in the brain wake it up to action, they spurt activities that generate the pleasure of productivity and/or the [pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning"). A healthy human being, in conditions of minimal welfare, in the morning, is on a happy trajectory to new happy acts and new happy learning. Life is and should be fun.

Sadly, this is not the case for a large proportion of youth. The mere act of being woken up against the biological clock and the need to go to the most hated place on earth - school - is enough to ruin the day. However, even on a good day, a person with a shattered reward-penalty balance may naturally seek news in the area of his prime worries and anxieties. Finding new knowledge is rewarding, but the new knowledge may build a vision of the world based on gloom and doom. If you fear cancer, learning about cancer may feel rewarding, and yet it adds to the data bank of scary scenarios that only magnify the fears. The brain tipped towards depressed mood may magnify the significance of depressing facts. This will particularly be noticeable in the interaction with social media. The positive feedback loop will spiral towards despair (see: [Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Gray:_Limits_on_freedom_lead_to_depression_and_suicide "Gray: Limits on freedom lead to depression and suicide")). School kills [resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resilience "Resilience").

While science of cancer is full of solutions that should provide a degree of protective shell for an anxious mind, social media is rife with fake news or alarmist news that only magnify the fears. Instead of adding to happy learning, the feedback loop of the algorithms magnifies fears and anxieties. It is the opposite effect of the one observed in a healthy individual.

[Coercive schooling](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_schooling "Coercive schooling") is a prime reason for the decline of [resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resilience "Resilience") in youth. This tips the balance towards the harm of social media

## Bipolar oscillations

Both manic and depressive states have runaway tendencies. A healthy happy state of the mind boosted with the [pleasure of learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Pleasure_of_learning "Pleasure of learning") and explosive [creativity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Creativity "Creativity") also shows that runaway based on positive feedback. Luckily, we do not go loco with euphoria as there are forces that keep that nuclear reactor of mental energy in check. One is fatigue; the other is adversity. On a good day, even the greatest elation will bring [homeostatic](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Homeostatic "Homeostatic") fatigue that gets magnified by [circadian](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Circadian "Circadian") lows resulting in [sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep "Sleep"). On a bad day, adversity strikes. It makes good sense to face adversity after a dose of positive feedback of high productivity. This provides a shell of [resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Resilience "Resilience") that facilitates resolution.

In a depressive loop, the runaway feedback process drags the mind into the pits of despair. Fortunately, it does not end up with suicide or utter inaction. The brain in a depressive state is also subject to fatigue. It will also deplete the stores of neurotransmitters. In the long run, it may also [exterminate connectivity](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/War_of_the_networks "War of the networks") that generates most traumatic thoughts.

Limited brain resources terminate the feedback loops of creative energy

At the lowest low, a patient may deplete all neural resources responsible for the runaway. In addition, the relative value of reward becomes disproportional. A hypothesis says that those two factors may trigger a manic state. The opposite happens when a manic state triggers a depressive episode. Such oscillations form the basis of the bipolar disorder, however, on a small scale, those processes affect everyone. For example, it is very hard to [replicate a very successful day](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Simple_formula_for_happiness "Simple formula for happiness") in terms of the emotional perception of success.

Feedback loops involved in high-octane learning may result in oscillations of bipolar nature

## Brain health

In a healthy mind, maximization of the euphoria associated with learning and creativity is harmless. It does not pose the risk of addiction (see: [Addiction to learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Addiction_to_learning "Addiction to learning")). It does not pose the risk of bipolar oscillations. The brain starts the day at the baseline level of contentment, builds up creative energy in the learning process, and naturally slows down with mounting fatigue.

To preserve that healthy and happy status quo, the following conditions need to be met:

- respect for [sleep](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Sleep "Sleep")
- respect for the [natural creativity cycle](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Natural_creativity_cycle "Natural creativity cycle")
- respect for learning freedom (as opposed to [coercive learning](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Coercive_learning "Coercive learning"))
- respect for the art of [stress resilience](https://supermemo.guru/wiki/Stress_resilience "Stress resilience")

Mental health is easy to keep with a bit of respect for freedom, sleep and own needs
